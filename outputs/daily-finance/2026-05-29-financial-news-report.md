## Key Financial News

- **Record U.S. close:** All three major indexes hit intraday all-time highs on May 29. The S&P 500 notched its ninth consecutive weekly gain, finishing May up ~5%; the Nasdaq closed the month up more than 8% — its best monthly return in years. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-may-29-2026))
- **Dell surges 33%:** Dell Technologies posted its best single-day gain on record after Q1 results beat on both revenue and earnings and management lifted full-year guidance, signalling continued AI-driven infrastructure demand. Micron rose ~5% and Qualcomm ~3% in sympathy. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/stock-market-today-live-updates.html))
- **U.S.–Iran ceasefire extension:** U.S. negotiators agreed to a 60-day memorandum of understanding to extend the ongoing ceasefire and begin talks on Iran's nuclear programme, pending President Trump's signature. The prospect of restored vessel flows through the Strait of Hormuz lifted equities and weighed on oil. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/oil-prices-iran-ceasefire-us-trump-strait-hormuz-energy-costs.html))
- **PCE inflation surprises higher:** April headline PCE rose 3.8% year-over-year (up from 3.5% in March), while core PCE came in at 3.3%. Real consumer spending grew just 0.1% month-on-month after inflation adjustment. The data dims near-term Fed rate-cut prospects. ([BEA / RISMedia](https://www.rismedia.com/2026/05/29/pce-report-inflation-up-april-rates-likely-wont-come-down/))
- **Fed on hold:** The Federal Funds rate remains at 3.50–3.75% following the April 28–29 FOMC meeting. With PCE above target and a new Fed Chair being selected following Jerome Powell's term expiry, monetary policy direction is in focus.
- **Oil's worst May in years:** WTI crude fell ~17% in May — its worst monthly decline since April 2025 — as Iran ceasefire optimism eased supply-risk premiums. WTI closed at $87.36/bbl on May 29, down 1.73% on the day. Brent posted its biggest monthly loss in six years. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/oil-price-iran-deal-war-ceasefire-trump.html))
- **ASX 200 rebounds:** After tumbling 1.4% on May 28, the ASX 200 bounced 1.43% to 8,716 on May 29, tracking overnight gains on Wall Street and buoyed by energy and tech-linked stocks. ([Market Index / IBTimes AU](https://www.ibtimes.com.au/asx-200-falls-global-energy-concerns-domestic-pressures-1869595))
- **Iron ore under pressure:** Iron ore 62% Fe futures were roughly flat near $106.80/T; the broader complex is tracking for a monthly decline amid a coal-cost spike pressuring Chinese steelmaker margins. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-29/iron-ore-heads-for-monthly-loss-as-coal-spike-pressures-margins))

## Investment Implications

- Sticky inflation (PCE at 3.8%) effectively closes the door on near-term Fed rate cuts. Fixed-income investors should brace for yields staying elevated; equity valuations remain stretched if rates do not fall.
- Dell's blowout quarter reinforces that AI capital-expenditure cycles remain intact — hardware, data-centre, and semiconductor names may continue to benefit even as broader macro uncertainty persists.
- The Iran ceasefire MOU, if signed by Trump, could structurally reduce the geopolitical risk premium in energy markets, keeping oil prices subdued and easing cost-push inflation pressures globally.

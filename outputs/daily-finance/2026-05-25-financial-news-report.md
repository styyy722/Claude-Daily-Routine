# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-05-25

## Executive Summary
- US equity markets were closed for Memorial Day; the S&P 500 finished Friday at a record 7,473.47, completing its eighth consecutive week of gains.
- Oil futures slumped nearly 6% to below $91/bbl on growing optimism that the US and Iran are nearing a deal to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, lifting global risk appetite.
- Australia's ASX 200 advanced 0.4% to 8,691, led by gold miners and airlines, while investors awaited Australia's April CPI (due May 27) after March inflation hit 4.6%.

## Key Financial News

- **US Memorial Day closure:** NYSE, Nasdaq, and US bond markets were shut for the federal holiday. Regular trading resumes Tuesday 26 May. Futures markets operated on abbreviated hours before resuming Sunday evening. ([investingLive](https://investinglive.com/stock-market-update/us-markets-face-shortened-friday-and-full-monday-closure-for-memorial-day-20260522/))
- **S&P 500 eighth straight weekly gain:** The index closed at a fresh record of 7,473.47 on 22 May, extending a winning streak not seen since 2024. The Dow also set a record at 50,579.70. ([Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/coverage/stock-market-today/2026/05/22/stock-market-today-may-22-s-and-p-500-reaches-eighth-straight-week-of-gains/))
- **Oil plunges on Iran deal progress:** WTI crude futures fell nearly 6% to below $91/bbl on 25 May as US and Iranian officials signalled progress toward a ceasefire and reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, through which ~20% of global oil and LNG flows. Brent also retreated sharply. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-25/emerging-market-assets-rise-as-oil-drops-on-us-iran-deal-hopes)) ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/22/oil-prices-today-trump-iran-strait-of-hormuz-uranium-.html))
- **Kevin Warsh sworn in as new Fed Chair:** Warsh was sworn in by Justice Thomas at the White House on 22 May, succeeding Jerome Powell. Senate confirmed him 54–45. He inherits an economy where fuel-driven inflation has remained elevated. ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/economy/2026/5/22/kevin-warsh-sworn-in-as-new-us-fed-chair)) ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/13/kevin-warsh-wins-senate-confirmation-as-the-next-federal-reserve-chair.html))
- **RBA hikes to 4.35%:** The Reserve Bank of Australia raised its cash rate target by 25 bps to 4.35% at its May meeting — the third consecutive hike of 2026 — driven by fuel-cost inflation tied to the Iran conflict. CBA economists expect the RBA to pause for the remainder of the year. ([CommBank](https://www.commbank.com.au/articles/newsroom/2026/05/rba-may-interest-rates-decision.html))
- **ASX 200 lifted by miners and airlines:** The index rose 34 pts to 8,691. Qantas surged 5.7% on lower jet-fuel cost expectations; gold miners Northern Star (+5.4%) and Evolution Mining (+4.5%) rallied as gold rose ~1.2%; BHP gained 0.7%. Most major banks also inched higher. ([Motley Fool AU](https://www.fool.com.au/2026/05/25/5-things-to-watch-on-the-asx-200-on-monday-25-may-2026/))
- **Gold rises, dollar softens:** Spot gold climbed to ~$4,563/oz (+1.18%) as the US dollar weakened on Iran deal optimism and lower oil prices eased global inflation fears. ([150currency.com](https://www.150currency.com/))

## Investment Implications

- A credible Strait of Hormuz reopening would ease energy-price inflation globally, potentially giving the RBA room to pause after its May hike and reducing pressure on the Fed's new chair to prioritise rate hikes over growth.
- Sustained US equity record highs alongside falling oil prices signal a "soft-landing" optimism trade, but elevated 10-year yields (~4.56%) continue to cap valuations, especially for growth stocks.
- Australian investors face near-term uncertainty from the April CPI release (27 May): a print above 5% could revive RBA rate-hike bets, pressuring rate-sensitive sectors such as REITs and consumer discretionary.

## Watchlist for Today

- **US markets reopen (Tuesday 26 May):** Watch for gap moves at open as equity futures and oil have shifted materially during the holiday; expect volatility in energy and travel stocks.
- **Australia April CPI (27 May):** Inflation expectations have risen to 5.9% — a hot print could undermine the case for an RBA pause and pressure the AUD and local bonds.
- **US-Iran peace negotiations:** Any formal announcement of a ceasefire or Hormuz deal would likely trigger a further oil sell-off and a broad emerging-market rally, while prolonged uncertainty could reverse Monday's gains.

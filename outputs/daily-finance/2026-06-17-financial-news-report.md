# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-17

## Executive Summary
- The US Federal Reserve held rates at 3.50%–3.75% at Chair Kevin Warsh's inaugural FOMC meeting, but nine of eighteen members signalled at least one rate hike is appropriate in 2026, sending the S&P 500 down 1.21% and Nasdaq lower by 1.34%.
- Oil prices slid further toward $77/bbl, down ~9% on the week, as the US and Iran moved toward signing an interim peace agreement expected to reopen the Strait of Hormuz.
- Australia's ASX 200 bucked the trend, closing up 0.54% to 8,966.3 ahead of the Fed decision, led by financials and gold miners while energy stocks fell sharply.

## Key Financial News

- **Fed holds, but signals rate hikes ahead:** The FOMC voted unanimously to hold the benchmark rate at 3.50%–3.75%, but Chair Kevin Warsh's first meeting produced a dramatically shorter policy statement and a hawkish dot plot — nine of eighteen members now favour at least one hike in 2026. The median year-end rate projection rose to 3.8%, up from 3.4% in March. Traders moved to price a potential October hike. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/17/nx-s1-5860084/fed-chief-warsh-first-fomc-meeting))
- **US equities sell off on hawkish Fed:** Major indices reversed intraday gains after the FOMC statement. S&P 500 -1.21% to 7,420.10; Dow Jones -0.98% (-507 pts) to 51,492.55; Nasdaq Composite -1.34% to 26,021.66. Technology led losses — Microsoft, Meta Platforms, Alphabet, and Amazon all closed lower, dragging the IT sector down 2.32%. ([The Street](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-17-2026))
- **Strong US retail sales fuel inflation concerns:** May retail sales rose 0.9%, more than doubling April's 0.4% gain and well above the 0.5% consensus. Ex-autos, sales still gained 0.8% vs 0.4% forecast — data that reinforces the case for tighter monetary policy in a context of four consecutive months of above-forecast inflation. ([Charles Schwab](https://www.schwab.com/learn/story/stock-market-update-open))
- **Oil falls as US–Iran deal nears signing:** WTI crude slid to ~$77/bbl, down close to 9% on the week, as both nations progressed toward an interim peace agreement with signature expected in Switzerland on Friday. The deal includes reopening the Strait of Hormuz and lifting sanctions on Iranian oil exports. Energy stocks on the ASX fell more than 2%. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/12/oil-prices-wti-brent-on-hopes-of-us-iran-deal-despite-tehran-pushback.html), [IG International](https://www.ig.com/en/news-and-trade-ideas/oil-falls-on-us-iran-deal-260616))
- **Bond yields surge:** The US 10-year Treasury yield rose to ~4.46%–4.50%, a multi-week high, reflecting the Fed's hawkish turn and upward revision to rate projections. Yield moves underscored the market's recalibration from a cuts path to a potential hikes path.
- **ASX 200 outperformed ahead of Fed:** Australia's benchmark gained 48.6 points (+0.54%) to 8,966.3, with gold stocks bouncing and Macquarie shares topping $250 for the first time. The All Ordinaries advanced 0.6% to 9,185.9. The session pre-dated the US Fed decision, leaving ASX futures exposed to overnight US weakness.
- **Iron ore pressured by China weakness and rising supply:** Dalian iron ore futures fell 1.90% to CNY 747.50/T (~$104/T USD), a two-month low, as Chinese crude steel output declined 2.7% year-on-year in May and Guinea's Simandou mine ramped production to 2.2 million tonnes in May from 1.3 million in April.

## Investment Implications

- **Higher-for-longer rates are back on the agenda:** With nine FOMC members favouring a hike and markets pricing October as a live meeting, rate-sensitive sectors — growth tech, real estate, utilities — face headwinds. Duration risk in bond portfolios has increased notably.
- **Oil supply shift may ease inflation, but slowly:** A reopened Hormuz could bring sustained relief to energy prices, reducing headline CPI and potentially giving the Fed pause before hiking. However, a full recovery of Iranian output could take months to years, limiting the near-term supply boost.
- **ASX faces a difficult open on 18 June:** The US market's post-Fed selloff, combined with pressure on iron ore and energy, points to a negative open for Australian miners (BHP, RIO, FMG) and the broader index despite the prior day's outperformance.

## Watchlist for Today

- **Fed Chair Warsh's post-FOMC commentary:** Any public remarks will be scrutinised for further guidance on the timing and magnitude of any 2026 rate hike — October is now the key date in focus.
- **US–Iran deal signing in Switzerland:** Confirmation of a signed MOU and specific Hormuz reopening timelines will directly drive oil price movements and determine the trajectory for global energy stocks.
- **ASX 200 open and major miners:** Watch BHP, RIO, and Fortescue for price action following overnight US weakness and the continued slide in iron ore — energy stocks may also extend losses if oil continues lower.

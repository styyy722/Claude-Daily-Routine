## Key Financial News

- **Dow hits fresh record:** The Dow Jones rose 182.60 points (+0.36%) to a record close of 50,644.28, with consumer staples and discretionary stocks leading gains — P&G (+3.23%), Nike (+2.79%), and Home Depot (+2.39%).
- **Oil slumps on Hormuz breakthrough:** WTI crude fell approximately 6% to ~$88.39/bbl after Iranian state television said Tehran is committed to restoring commercial shipping through the Strait of Hormuz to pre-war levels within a month. ([FX Daily Report](https://fxdailyreport.com/wti-crude-oil-price-analysis-for-may-27-2026/))
- **Goldman raises S&P 500 target to 8,000:** Goldman Sachs lifted its 2026 year-end S&P 500 forecast to 8,000 from 7,600, citing continued AI-driven productivity growth and easing inflation expectations. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/investing/goldman-sachs-doubles-down-on-sp-500-message-for-2026-raise-target-index))
- **JPMorgan retreats on M&A comment:** JPMorgan shares fell ~2% after CEO Jamie Dimon stated the bank could spend up to $20 billion on an acquisition "in the next couple of years," raising concerns about capital deployment.
- **ASX 200 softer, AUD falls:** Australia's ASX 200 closed down 0.4% at 8,658, dragged by the energy sector amid lower oil prices. AUD/USD declined 0.53% to 0.7133 as the USD firmed on the day.
- **Gold retreats:** Gold fell ~$67 to approximately $4,452/oz as falling oil prices reduced inflation risk premiums and US real yields remained elevated.
- **US Treasury yields ease slightly:** The 10-year Treasury yield was ~4.48%, slightly lower on the day as oil-led disinflation prospects tempered rate expectations heading into the April PCE report.

## Investment Implications

- The Iran/Hormuz development is materially bearish for energy stocks and oil-linked currencies (AUD, CAD, NOK) but provides a significant tailwind for consumer spending and transport-intensive sectors.
- Goldman's target upgrade and record-setting US indices suggest the market is pricing in a soft-landing scenario; investors should watch whether the April PCE data confirms or challenges this view.
- A lower oil price environment combined with easing yields may provide relief for rate-sensitive sectors (real estate, utilities, growth tech), but AUD weakness warrants monitoring for Australian equity funds with unhedged USD exposure.

## Watchlist for Today (2026-05-28)

- **April PCE data** (US Personal Income and Outlays): Market consensus is watching for signs that core PCE held near 2.5%–3.0%, which could validate or challenge Goldman's bullish outlook.
- **Energy sector response:** Watch for follow-through oil price moves and any further Iran/Hormuz updates that could swiftly reverse Wednesday's selloff.
- **ASX 200 open & AUD/USD:** With US equities at record highs, ASX investors will weigh oil-driven sectoral headwinds against the positive global equities backdrop.

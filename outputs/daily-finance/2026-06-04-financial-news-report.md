# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-04

## Executive Summary
- Broadcom (AVGO) plunged ~14% after its Q3 AI chip revenue guidance of $16B missed the $17.2B analyst consensus, triggering a broad semiconductor rout with the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index down over 6%.
- US markets showed sharp sector rotation: the Dow Jones surged to a new all-time record as investors pivoted to Financials (+2.67%), Health Care (+3.14%), and Real Estate (+1.87%), while the S&P 500 edged up 0.41% and the Nasdaq barely closed flat (-0.09%).
- Australia's ASX 200 fell 1.23% with BHP (-3.2%) and Rio Tinto (-3.7%) retreating from recent highs as iron ore prices slipped on weak Chinese demand, while an Israel-Lebanon ceasefire eased oil prices and gold held firm near $4,479/oz.

## Key Financial News

- **Broadcom selloff dominates tech narrative:** AVGO dropped ~14% to around $410 after fiscal Q2 results beat expectations but Q3 AI chip revenue guidance of $16B missed the $17.2B analyst consensus. Broadcom also maintained its full-year AI chip sales target of $100B rather than raising it. AMD fell ~4% and Intel ~3% in sympathy; the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index lost more than 6%. CrowdStrike (CRWD) also declined sharply on its own post-earnings update. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/04/broadcoms-outlook-sparked-a-selloff-for-chip-stocks-heres-why.html), [247WallSt](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/06/04/broadcom-sinks-14-on-soft-ai-chip-outlook-despite-earnings-beat-dragging-down-amd-and-intel/))

- **Dow Jones hits new record via sector rotation:** Investors rotated aggressively from chip stocks into defensive and cyclical sectors, driving the Dow Jones to a fresh all-time high. Health Care (+3.14%), Financials (+2.67%), and Real Estate (+1.87%) led. Eight of the S&P 500's 11 sectors closed higher. ([TradeKey](https://www.tradingkey.com/analysis/stocks/us-stocks/261946941-avgo-broadcom-q2-dowjones-nasdaq-ai-stock-tradingkey))

- **ASX 200 retreats on materials selloff:** Australia's benchmark fell 1.23% to 8,677, reversing the prior session's gains. BHP Group shed 3.2% and Rio Tinto fell 3.7% after Broadcom's after-hours release filtered into overnight sentiment. Iron ore's slide on weak China demand and high port inventories compounded the move. ([Motley Fool AU](https://www.fool.com.au/2026/06/04/5-things-to-watch-on-the-asx-200-on-thursday-4-june-2026/))

- **Israel-Lebanon ceasefire eases energy risk premium:** An agreed ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon reduced the geopolitical risk premium in oil markets, pulling WTI crude down to around $93.10/bbl. Concerns about the broader Middle East conflict and Strait of Hormuz access continue to keep energy prices elevated relative to pre-conflict levels. ([FXStreet](https://www.fxstreet.com/markets/commodities/metals/gold))

- **ADP jobs data strengthens Fed rate hike bets:** ADP's May private-sector payrolls report showed +122K, above forecasts and the highest reading since January 2025. Markets now price in an 85% probability of a Fed rate hike by year-end, up from ~60% a week prior. The higher rate path supports the US dollar and keeps yield pressure on rate-sensitive assets. ([Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/government-bond-yield))

- **US 10-year Treasury yield holds near 4.49%:** The benchmark 10-year yield dipped a marginal 1bp to 4.49%, with the 10Y-2Y spread at +41bp and the 10Y-3M spread at +78bp, reflecting a gradually normalising yield curve as Fed hike pricing builds. ([FRED / St. Louis Fed](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/DGS10))

- **Gold recovers toward $4,500:** Gold rose 1.00% to $4,479/oz as reduced geopolitical tensions softened the USD's safe-haven premium, partially supporting the metal. Broader macro uncertainty and elevated inflation expectations continue to underpin gold at historically elevated levels. ([GoldPrice.org](https://goldprice.org/))

## Investment Implications

- Broadcom's guidance miss casts doubt on the pace of AI infrastructure spending; investors may reassess valuations in high-multiple semiconductor names that have driven the 2026 rally, with near-term downside risk for AI-exposed ETFs and chip indices.
- The Dow's record high amid the tech selloff indicates broad-based economic resilience; a sustained rotation into Financials and Health Care could extend the Dow's outperformance versus the Nasdaq if AI enthusiasm moderates.
- For Australian investors, the ASX 200 materials selloff in BHP and Rio Tinto may create medium-term entry opportunities, but weak Chinese iron ore demand and the AUD trading near 0.7134 warrant caution until clearer demand signals emerge from China.

## Watchlist for Today

- **Broadcom and chip sector stabilisation:** Watch for analyst upgrades and retail buying interest in AVGO and the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index — a floor in chip names would likely help the Nasdaq recover ground.
- **US Non-Farm Payrolls (due June 6):** With ADP at +122K and Fed rate hike pricing surging to 85%, the official May jobs report will be the dominant near-term catalyst for yields, the USD, and equity risk appetite.
- **ASX 200 materials recovery:** BHP and Rio Tinto's direction will track overnight iron ore and copper spot prices; any sign of stabilising Chinese demand could prompt a swift reversal of Thursday's losses.

# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-08

*Note: 8 June 2026 is a Monday. The ASX 200 was closed for the King's Birthday public holiday in most Australian states. This report covers the US Monday session and key overnight developments.*

## Executive Summary
- US equities rebounded on Monday as chip stocks surged — semiconductor names recovered sharply after Friday's ~$1 trillion rout, with the Philadelphia SE Semiconductor Index rising 6.7%, led by Marvell (+14%) and Intel (+12.3%).
- Middle East de-escalation offered tentative relief: Iran declared its latest military operation in Israel "over" and Israel halted strikes at President Trump's request, though the broader Strait of Hormuz standoff remained unresolved and oil pushed higher.
- Treasury yields continued to climb toward 4.57% — a multi-week high — as Friday's blowout May jobs report (+172K vs. 85K forecast) cemented ~70% market odds of a Fed rate hike by December 2026, weighing on gold and rate-sensitive assets.

## Key Financial News

- **Chip Stocks Lead Wall Street Rebound:** The Philadelphia SE Semiconductor Index surged 6.7% as the sector clawed back a portion of Friday's near-$1 trillion rout. Marvell Technology jumped ~14% after confirming it will join the S&P 500 on 22 June; Intel rallied ~12.3% following reports that Alphabet's Google placed an order to manufacture more than three million tensor processing units in 2028; Micron gained ~9%; and Nvidia rose ~2% after CEO Jensen Huang described Friday's selloff as "a buying opportunity." ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-monday-june-8-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-jump-085310403.html) | [TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-08-2026))

- **Iran Declares Military Operation Over; Israel Halts Strikes:** Iran's military announced that its first wave of strikes on Israel since April's ceasefire had concluded, and Israel halted retaliatory operations at the direct request of President Trump. The apparent pause in direct exchanges lifted risk sentiment, though Iran has yet to lift its threat to close the Strait of Hormuz and indirect ceasefire negotiations remain stalled. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/p-500-nasdaq-futures-climb-102103095.html))

- **Oil Higher Despite Tentative De-escalation:** Despite the pause in Iran–Israel strikes, WTI crude rose ~2.8% to around $91/bbl (Brent ~$94/bbl) as the Strait of Hormuz risk premium remained elevated. Analysts note that ceasefire talks are still fragile and that Iranian shipping restrictions in the Gulf have not been lifted. ([Motley Fool AU](https://www.fool.com.au/2026/06/09/5-things-to-watch-on-the-asx-200-on-tuesday-09-june-2026/))

- **Treasury Yields Hit Multi-Week High; Rate Hike Bets Firm:** The 10-year US Treasury yield climbed to ~4.57% on Monday, extending Friday's jobs-driven surge. Following the May payrolls beat (+172K vs. ~85K forecast), markets now price approximately 70% probability of a 25bp Fed rate hike by December 2026 — up from ~50% pre-data — while incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's debut FOMC meeting on 16–17 June is widely expected to deliver a hold but potentially scrap the "dot plot" forward guidance. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/policy/articles/traders-fully-price-fed-rate-124726318.html) | [Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/government-bond-yield))

- **Apple Fades After WWDC Pop:** Apple shares initially rose ~3% during the WWDC developer keynote on Monday but ended the day down more than 1% as investors expressed scepticism about the timeline and practical impact of the AI features announced, tempering broader tech enthusiasm outside semiconductors.

- **AUD/USD Edges Higher; RBA Decision Looms:** The Australian dollar firmed slightly to ~0.7058, up ~0.14% on the day, as the partial easing of Middle East tensions modestly supported risk appetite. Attention is turning to the RBA's 16 June meeting — the cash rate sits at 4.35% following a 25bp hike in May — with economists divided between a pause and a further hike, given headline inflation forecast to peak at ~4.8% in the June quarter. ([RBA](https://www.rba.gov.au/media-releases/2026/mr-26-12.html) | [Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/australia/currency))

- **Iron Ore Remains Under Pressure:** Iron ore futures held near the lower end of recent ranges at roughly CNY 759/T (~US$102–104/T), as abundant supply from Australia and Brazil and elevated Chinese port inventories continued to weigh on prices. Steel mill profitability in China has declined and blast-furnace utilisation is broadly steady, offering little demand-side support. ([Trading Economics – Iron Ore](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/iron-ore))

## Investment Implications

- **Chip Rebound May Be Tactical, Not Structural:** Monday's semiconductor rally came on low volume holiday context (ASX closed, many Asian markets reduced staffing) and was concentrated in idiosyncratic catalysts (Google TPU order, S&P 500 inclusions). With the 10-year yield pressing ~4.57%, real rates remain elevated and growth-stock multiples remain pressured — the sector's fundamentals argue for continued volatility rather than a clean trend reversal.
- **Middle East Risk Premium Likely Sticky:** Even with Monday's ceasefire pause, the Strait of Hormuz threat has not been formally rescinded and Iran–US talks remain suspended. Oil could move sharply in either direction on a single headline — with WTI already back above $91, a breakdown in talks could push prices toward prior 2026 highs while a comprehensive deal could unwind the premium quickly.
- **Australian Investors Return to a Busy Week:** The ASX re-opens Tuesday to a backdrop of a modest Wall Street rebound, firmer oil, and rising global bond yields — likely a mixed open. Key local data (consumer and business confidence) and an upcoming RBA decision (16 June) will determine whether domestic rate-sensitive sectors can stabilise after last week's slide.

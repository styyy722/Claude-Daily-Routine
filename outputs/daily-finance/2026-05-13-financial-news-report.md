## Key Financial News

- **Hot PPI data:** The US Bureau of Labor Statistics reported that the Producer Price Index rose 1.4% month-over-month and 6.0% year-over-year in April 2026 — the highest annual reading since December 2022. Over 75% of the monthly gain stemmed from a 7.8% surge in energy prices, with gasoline alone jumping 15.6%, driven by the Iran War and ongoing tariff pressures. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/13/ppi-inflation-report-april-2026-.html), [BLS](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/pdf/ppi.pdf))

- **Equities diverge:** Tech stocks led the S&P 500 and Nasdaq to new record closes despite the inflation scare, as investors appeared willing to look through short-term energy-driven PPI spikes. The Dow Jones lagged, dragged by communication and financial stocks. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-may-13-2026-updates))

- **Treasury yields spike:** The 10-year Treasury yield rose to ~4.49%, its highest level since July 2025. The 20-year and 30-year yields both eclipsed 5% for the first time since May 2025. Rate-cut odds for 2026 fell sharply, with market pricing now assigning around a 39% chance of a hike. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-13/us-10-year-treasury-yield-hits-highest-since-july-after-ppi-data))

- **Fed on hold:** The Federal Reserve held rates steady at 3.5%–3.75% for its third consecutive meeting, upgrading its language to describe inflation as "elevated" (previously "somewhat elevated"). Policymakers are weighing the dual impact of Iran War energy shocks and residual tariff pass-through. ([Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260318a.htm))

- **RBA tightens further:** The Reserve Bank of Australia raised the cash rate 25 bps to 4.35% at its May meeting — its third consecutive hike — after underlying CPI surged to 4.6% in March. Governor Michele Bullock warned inflation risks are "tilted to the upside" and flagged Iran War energy spillovers as a key concern. ([RBA](https://www.rba.gov.au/media-releases/2026/mr-26-08.html), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/05/australia-central-bank-rate-hike-inflation-rba.html))

- **ASX 200 slides:** The Australian benchmark fell 0.46% to 8,630.40, extending a three-session losing streak as fading hopes for a US–Iran peace deal and cautious US futures weighed on sentiment. Aristocrat Leisure (+13.28%) was the standout gainer after posting strong half-year results. ([Motley Fool AU](https://www.fool.com.au/2026/05/13/here-are-the-top-10-asx-200-shares-today-13-may-2026/))

- **Oil and commodities:** WTI crude held above $102/bbl, with the IEA reporting global observed oil stockpiles fell ~4 million barrels per day in March and April. Iron ore slipped 0.28% to ~$111.11/T but remains up ~11.7% year-on-year. Gold traded around $4,710/oz, softening slightly as rising real yields reduce its appeal.

## Investment Implications

- **Inflation persistence is the dominant theme:** With PPI running at 6.0% YoY and CPI above 3.8%, both the Fed and RBA are firmly in "higher for longer" mode. Bonds and rate-sensitive sectors (REITs, utilities) face continued headwinds while energy stocks benefit from elevated crude.
- **Tech resilience vs. rate risk:** The S&P 500 and Nasdaq's ability to push to new records despite surging yields reflects strong earnings expectations in AI and mega-cap tech, but this divergence raises concentration risk — any earnings disappointment could trigger a sharper correction.
- **AUD faces competing forces:** The RBA's hawkish tilt is supportive of AUD/USD, but slowing Chinese demand (reflected in softer iron ore) and global risk aversion from the Iran conflict could cap upside near 0.73.

## Watchlist for Today

- **US CPI (April 2026):** Due shortly after PPI, CPI data will confirm whether consumer-level inflation is also re-accelerating. A hot print could push yields higher and rattle equities further.
- **ASX 200 open:** Watch for further selling pressure given elevated oil prices and weak US futures overnight; resources and energy stocks will be key movers.
- **Fed speakers:** Multiple FOMC members are scheduled to speak; any hawkish commentary reinforcing a rate-hike possibility (currently ~39%) could further steepen the yield curve.

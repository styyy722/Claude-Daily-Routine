## Key Financial News

- **Trump rejects Iran deal:** President Trump called Iran's latest peace response "totally unacceptable," dashing hopes for a diplomatic resolution and keeping the Strait of Hormuz effectively closed. Oil prices rallied ~2% on the news, with Brent crude topping $104–105/bbl. Oil is up ~60% since the Iran conflict began in late February. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/11/gold-falls-on-oil-driven-inflation-worries-as-usiran-peace-talks-falter.html))

- **Semiconductor and AI stocks lead US gains:** Chip stocks extended their rally, with Micron (MU) surging to a record high. JPMorgan Private Bank declared the "AI supercycle may just be getting started," backing semiconductor supply chains and data centre infrastructure. The memory-focused DRAM ETF surpassed $6.5bn in assets faster than any prior ETF launch. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-monday-may-11-oil-rise-trump-rejects-peace-225625559.html))

- **ASX 200 falls 1.5%:** Australian equities dropped 134 points to 8,744, ending two days of gains amid broad profit-taking and caution ahead of Chinese inflation data. Healthcare, consumer durables, and financials led losses; the big four banks fell 1.8–3.3%. Energy and process industries provided partial offsets. ([Market Index](https://www.marketindex.com.au/asx200))

- **Fed holds rates, market dials back cut expectations:** The FOMC kept the federal funds rate unchanged at 3.50–3.75% at its April 29 meeting. Bond markets are no longer pricing any 2026 rate cuts, with growing internal FOMC disagreement about whether to even signal an easing bias. US 10-year yields rose to 4.41%; 10-year JGBs climbed to 2.51% and 30-year JGBs broke above 3.76%. ([Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm))

- **US April jobs: solid but cooling:** The US economy added 115,000 jobs in April, above forecasts but down from March's revised 185,000. Michigan Consumer Sentiment hit a record low of 48.2, reflecting household unease over fuel prices and tariffs; year-ahead inflation expectations eased slightly to 4.5% from 4.7%. ([Saxo](https://www.home.saxo/content/articles/macro/market-quick-take---11-may-2026-11052026))

- **China trade surplus hits record:** China's exports surged 14.1% year-over-year to a record $359.4bn in April, driven by component stockpiling ahead of potential tariff escalation. Iron ore firmed more than 3% as post-Labour Day holiday restocking by Chinese steel mills lifted prices back firmly above $100/t. ([Westpac IQ](https://www.westpaciq.com.au/economics/2026/05/commodities-update-may-2026))

- **Gold retreats on oil/inflation dynamics:** Gold fell to ~$4,742/oz as rising oil prices amplified inflation fears and drove up US real yields, dimming gold's appeal. Gold is down more than 11% since the Iran conflict began. Copper, however, hit a record close of $6.4605/lb, up over 2%, on strong demand signals. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/11/gold-falls-on-oil-driven-inflation-worries-as-usiran-peace-talks-falter.html))

## Investment Implications

- **Oil risk is the dominant macro variable:** With Brent above $100 and Iran talks broken down, energy costs are feeding directly into inflation expectations, complicating central bank paths globally. Portfolios with energy exposure (XLE, oil majors) may benefit, while rate-sensitive assets and consumer discretionary remain under pressure.
- **AI/semiconductor trade has further legs:** Institutional upgrades (JPMorgan, record ETF flows) and solid earnings momentum suggest the chip rally is not purely speculative. However, elevated valuations mean any inflation or rate shock could trigger sharp sector rotations.
- **ASX vulnerable to twin headwinds:** A lower AUD (0.7245) provides some export support, but falling bank stocks and China demand uncertainty weigh on the index. Iron ore above $100/t is a positive for BHP/RIO, but this could reverse quickly if Chinese stimulus disappoints.

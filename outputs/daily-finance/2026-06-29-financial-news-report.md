## Key Financial News

- **Alphabet joins the Dow; index tops 52,000**: Google parent Alphabet (GOOGL) officially entered the Dow Jones Industrial Average on June 29, replacing Verizon — marking the Dow's largest-ever tilt toward tech, with five of the Magnificent Seven now represented. Alphabet shares rose ~3.7% on debut; the Dow closed above 52,000 for the first time. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/alphabet-joins-dow-jones-index-170003693.html))
- **Tech leads broad market rally**: The Nasdaq added 2.07% as a relief rally swept through technology names following last week's AI-cost-driven selloff. Alphabet's index debut amplified optimism across the sector.
- **US–Iran tensions ease**: Both nations agreed to halt tit-for-tat attacks, with diplomatic talks scheduled in Doha, Qatar — reducing Middle East risk premiums in oil markets and supporting broader risk appetite. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-monday-june-29-224230573.html))
- **Supreme Court / Fed independence**: The US Supreme Court rejected the Trump administration's attempt to dismiss Federal Reserve Governor Lisa Cook, allowing her to remain in post. However, the ruling also broadened presidential firing authority over other independent agencies, keeping Fed governance risk on the radar. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-monday-june-29-224230573.html))
- **Fed rate on hold**: The federal funds rate remains at 3.50%–3.75%. All eyes shift to Thursday's June jobs report — moved from its usual Friday release because of the July 4 market closure — which is the next key macro catalyst for rate-cut expectations.
- **Oil rebounds on geopolitics**: WTI crude rose ~1.7% to approximately $69.70/bbl and Brent gained ~1.4% to ~$73/bbl on eased Middle East supply-risk fears linked to the US–Iran agreement. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-monday-june-29-224230573.html))
- **Gold eases, AUD/USD steady**: Gold slipped $11.10 to $4,027.80/oz (-0.27%) as improved risk sentiment reduced safe-haven demand. AUD/USD edged down 0.08% to 0.6890, consolidating after last week's weakness. Iron ore is approximately $100/T (CNY futures -0.27% on the day; USD figure approximate based on June 26 close of $100.33/T).

## Investment Implications

- Alphabet's entry into the Dow accelerates the index's concentration in AI and digital-economy stocks. The Dow is no longer a defensive counterweight to the Nasdaq — broad index moves will increasingly track Magnificent Seven performance.
- The Supreme Court's mixed ruling on Fed independence (protecting Cook's seat while broadening other dismissal powers) introduces a new dimension of institutional risk. Markets will watch whether the ruling emboldens further executive moves toward the Fed's leadership structure.
- Thursday's jobs report is the next critical macro data point. A strong print would push back rate-cut expectations and pressure duration-sensitive assets; a soft result could reignite rate-cut optimism and sustain the equity rally through the July 4 holiday.

- US equity markets closed mixed as surging Treasury yields and oil-driven inflation fears dragged the S&P 500 and Nasdaq lower, while the Dow edged up modestly.

- The RBA delivered its third consecutive 25bp rate hike (cash rate now 4.35%), sending the ASX 200 down 1.45% and reinforcing a hawkish monetary policy backdrop across developed economies.

## Key Financial News

- **US markets mixed on yield surge:** The Nasdaq fell 0.54% and S&P 500 fell 0.20% as rising bond yields pressured technology stocks, including Nvidia, Broadcom, and Intel. The Dow rose 0.19%, supported by defensive and energy sectors.
- **Treasury yields hit 1-year high:** The US 10-year yield climbed above 4.62% — its highest level in approximately a year — after CPI and PPI data showed oil-driven inflation feeding into broader prices, pushing traders to fully price out any Fed rate cuts in 2026.
- **Oil volatility on Iran tensions:** WTI crude surged to an intraday high of $111.34/bbl before retreating to $99.35 after President Trump signalled he would delay military action against Iran and that "serious negotiations are now taking place." Brent reached $107.72/bbl. The Strait of Hormuz remains a key flashpoint.
- **Gold edges up amid yield headwinds:** Gold gained 0.19% to $4,546.75/oz; upside was limited by a stronger US dollar and rising Treasury yields despite safe-haven demand from Middle East uncertainty.
- **RBA raises rates for third consecutive time:** The Reserve Bank of Australia raised its cash rate by 25bps to 4.35% in an 8-1 split vote, warning that inflation "is likely to remain above target for some time." CBA forecasts rates on hold for the rest of 2026, while Westpac expects two further hikes in June and August.
- **ASX 200 falls 1.45%:** The Australian index closed at 8,505.3, with BHP (-2.6%), Goodman Group (-2.7%), and Northern Star Resources (-4.1%) among the biggest drags. Brambles tumbled 14.9% after cutting its underlying profit growth guidance. Lynas Rare Earths rose 5.46% and the ASX Energy Index gained 2%.
- **Iron ore firmer on Chinese demand:** Iron ore rose to approximately $105.14/T, up around 5% over four weeks, as Chinese steel mills increased output amid recovering end-user consumption.

## Investment Implications

- **Rate hike cycle not over:** With both the RBA and markets pricing further Fed tightening, rate-sensitive sectors — technology, property, and growth stocks — face continued pressure. Investors may look to defensives and energy as hedges.
- **Oil remains a key macro risk:** Geopolitical volatility in the Middle East continues to keep oil prices elevated. Sustained high energy prices risk embedding broader inflation, complicating central bank decisions globally.
- **Earnings season test ahead:** Nvidia (Wednesday), Target, and Walmart results this week will be critical in determining whether AI-driven momentum and consumer spending can hold up under the weight of higher rates and inflation.

## Watchlist for Today (19 May 2026)

- **RBA communication:** Watch for any follow-up commentary from the RBA Governor on the pace of further tightening after the split vote.
- **Oil and Middle East headlines:** Trump's Iran negotiations remain fluid; any escalation or resolution will drive significant moves in energy prices and inflation expectations.
- **US bond market:** Monitor whether the 10-year yield sustains above 4.6%; a further move higher would intensify pressure on equities and riskier assets.

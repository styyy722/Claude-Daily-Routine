## Key Financial News

- **US Equities hit records:** The S&P 500 and Nasdaq both closed at all-time highs on Friday. The Nasdaq gained 5.5% for the week — its strongest weekly performance in months — with AI and semiconductor stocks leading. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/06/stock-market-today-live-updates.html))
- **Strong US payrolls:** April nonfarm payrolls came in at 115,000 vs. ~62,000 expected, with unemployment steady at 4.3% and wage growth modest at 0.2% m/m. The beat supported risk appetite while keeping Fed rate-hike speculation contained. ([Federal Reserve H.15](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h15/))
- **RBA hikes to 4.35%:** The RBA raised its cash rate 25 bps at its May meeting (third hike of 2026), citing Q1 CPI of 4.09% y/y and March inflation of 4.6% — the highest since monthly CPI data began. The Bank flagged Middle East–driven fuel costs as a key upside inflation risk. ([RBA Media Release](https://www.rba.gov.au/media-releases/2026/mr-26-08.html), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/05/australia-central-bank-rate-hike-inflation-rba.html))
- **ASX 200 snaps losing streak:** The ASX 200 ended the week +0.17% at 8,744.40, snapping a three-week losing streak. Mining and gold stocks provided support, though energy names were mixed on the oil slide. ([ASX 200 Mining Highs](https://discoveryalert.com.au/asx-200-mining-shares-multi-year-highs-commodities-2026/))
- **US–Iran deal hopes weigh on oil:** Reports that US and Iranian officials were nearing a peace framework drove Brent below $102 and WTI below $96. US jets earlier disabled Iranian oil tankers in the Strait of Hormuz, but diplomatic channels reportedly remain active. ([Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/business/2026/05/07/hopes-of-reopening-the-strait-of-hormuz-lift-shares-as-oil-remains-above-100), [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/business/business-news/oil-markets-gas-prices-450-us-iran-near-deal-end-war-hormuz-rcna343819))
- **Nvidia–Corning AI infrastructure deal:** Nvidia announced a long-term partnership with Corning to expand US optical connectivity capacity 10x and fibre production by 50%+, supporting AI data-centre buildout. Corning shares surged ~17% on the announcement. ([TradingKey](https://www.tradingkey.com/analysis/stocks/us-stocks/261862892-iran-deal-oil-gold-stocks-amd-nvidia-corning-forex-tradingkey))
- **Gold near recent highs, iron ore steady:** Gold climbed above $4,730/oz — its highest level since late April — as geopolitical uncertainty sustained safe-haven demand. Iron ore held at $110.93/t, up ~12.6% year-on-year. ([Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/gold))

## Investment Implications

- The RBA's third consecutive hike to 4.35% signals persistent domestic inflation pressure. Rate-sensitive sectors (property, consumer discretionary, REITs) face continued headwinds, while the AUD has strengthened against expectations — watch for further AUD/USD upside if the RBA signals more hikes.
- Oil's decline (if sustained on a genuine US–Iran deal) would ease a key inflationary driver globally and in Australia, potentially offering the RBA room to pause later in 2026. However, any breakdown in negotiations could quickly reverse the move.
- US equities are at record highs on six consecutive weeks of gains; stretched valuations and elevated Treasury yields (~4.39%) may cap near-term upside. AI-linked names (Nvidia, Corning) remain key momentum drivers — watch for signs of rotation.

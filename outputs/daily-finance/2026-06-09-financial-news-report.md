# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-09

## Executive Summary
- Wall Street swung sharply after Iran reportedly shot down a US Army Apache helicopter over the Strait of Hormuz with a Shahed drone; President Trump ordered "self-defense" strikes on Iran, sending the Nasdaq down as much as 3.5% intraday before a partial recovery (S&P 500 -0.26% to 7,386.65, Nasdaq ~-1.0% to 25,678.82, Dow +0.17% to 50,872.11).
- Oil fell despite the escalation, with WTI down 2.6% to ~$88/bbl and Brent -2% to ~$92/bbl, as traders looked past the immediate headline toward Wednesday's US CPI print — the last major data point before incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's 16–17 June FOMC debut.
- Gold slid 1.31% to ~$4,260/oz, its lowest level since late March, while the ASX 200 fell for a third straight session (-0.23% to 8,605.4) as miners weighed on the index amid softer iron ore/copper prices and China demand concerns, partly offset by resilient banks and tech.

## Key Financial News

- **US-Iran Escalation Roils Markets:** An Army Apache helicopter patrolling the Strait of Hormuz was shot down by an Iranian Shahed drone (its two crew were rescued by an uncrewed drone boat). President Trump said the US "must, of necessity, respond," and US Central Command carried out "proportional self-defense strikes" against Iran around 5pm ET. The Nasdaq fell as much as 3.5% intraday before paring losses to close down ~1%. ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/06/09/world/live-news/iran-war-trump-israel) | [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-tuesday-june-9-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-rise-ai-225640870.html))

- **Chip Rotation Resumes:** Monday's semiconductor rebound reversed as Nvidia, Micron and Broadcom all declined, with investors continuing to rotate out of AI-related names even as the broader market clawed back most of its intraday losses. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-tuesday-june-9-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-rise-ai-225640870.html))

- **Oil Eases Despite Tensions:** WTI crude fell 2.6% to ~$88/bbl and Brent dropped 2% to ~$92/bbl, reversing earlier intraday gains of 3–4%, as markets appeared to interpret the US strikes as a contained, proportional response rather than an open-ended escalation toward closing the Strait of Hormuz. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-tuesday-june-9-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-rise-ai-225640870.html))

- **Gold Hits Multi-Month Low:** Bullion fell 1.31% to ~$4,260/oz, its weakest level since late March, as a firmer US dollar and Treasury yields near 4.57% reflect building expectations of a Fed rate hike, with Wednesday's CPI report seen as the key swing factor. ([Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/gold))

- **ASX 200 Extends Losing Streak:** The benchmark fell 0.23% (-19.7 pts) to 8,605.4, its third consecutive decline. Miners including BHP, Rio Tinto and Fortescue dropped 1–2% on softer iron ore and copper prices and concerns over Chinese demand, while the big four banks were resilient and technology/growth names outperformed. ([IBTimes AU](https://www.ibtimes.com.au/asx-200-dips-amid-mining-energy-weakness-1870368))

- **AUD/USD Edges Lower:** The Australian dollar slipped 0.16% to 0.7028 amid broad US dollar strength and risk-off positioning ahead of US CPI. The RBA's 16 June decision remains a key focus given inflation forecast to run hot. ([Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/australia/currency))

- **Notable Company News:** Applied Digital surged more than 12% after announcing a $5.2 billion data-centre lease agreement with a major hyperscaler. Separately, SpaceX's IPO drew roughly $150 billion in institutional orders for $75 billion of available shares, marking one of the largest order books on record. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-tuesday-june-9-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-rise-ai-225640870.html))

## Investment Implications

- **Geopolitical Risk Remains Headline-Driven and Binary:** Oil's failure to rally on Tuesday's strikes suggests markets currently view the Apache response as contained. However, any sign of a broader disruption to the Strait of Hormuz — through which a large share of global oil and LNG transits — could reverse this quickly and sharply.
- **Rate Repricing Is the Dominant Cross-Asset Driver:** With the 10-year Treasury yield near 4.57% and gold at a 2.5-month low, Wednesday's US CPI print is the most important catalyst of the week and may shape expectations for incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's 16–17 June FOMC debut.
- **Rotation Favours Lower-Multiple Exposures:** The pullback in chip stocks alongside the ASX miners' underperformance versus banks and tech suggests investors are de-risking from the most rate- and momentum-sensitive names ahead of the CPI release.

## Watchlist for Wednesday, 10 June 2026

- **US CPI Report:** The key inflation print ahead of the Fed's 16–17 June meeting — a hot reading would cement rate-hike expectations and could pressure bonds, equities and gold further.

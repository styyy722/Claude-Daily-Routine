## Investment Implications

- **Iran escalation trajectory is the key risk variable for the week:** If Trump's ultimatum triggers renewed military action or further Strait disruption, oil could spike above $110/bbl, driving another leg higher in yields and a fresh sell-off in equities — particularly growth, consumer, and transport-exposed sectors.
- **RBA at 4.35% compresses Australian household budgets and equity multiples:** Three hikes in 2026 represent a cumulative 75 bps of tightening layered on top of the 2022–2024 cycle; variable-rate mortgage holders, consumer-discretionary ASX names, and REIT valuations face renewed downside if the market prices in Westpac's 4.85% scenario.
- **High yields favour defensives and cash over growth:** With the US 30-year at 5% and the 10-year at 4.59%, risk-adjusted returns from quality bonds are increasingly competitive with equities at current valuations; a rotation into investment-grade credit, utilities, and healthcare at the expense of speculative tech is the natural portfolio response.

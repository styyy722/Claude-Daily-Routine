- Wall Street closed a fifth straight losing session for the Nasdaq on Friday, June 26, as an AI-chip cost shock and a reported OpenAI IPO delay to 2027 hit tech and chip stocks hard.
- The RBA held its cash rate at 4.35% after three consecutive 2026 hikes, while the Fed signalled a more hawkish stance, pushing the US 10-year yield down to ~4.38% on rate-path uncertainty.
- Gold slid toward $4,000/oz (down ~5% for the week) and oil eased further as US–Iran peace efforts reduced Middle East supply-risk premiums; AUD/USD weakened to ~0.689.

## Key Financial News
- **Tech/AI selloff**: The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index sank 4.8% Friday; Samsung and SK Hynix fell over 8% and 9% respectively in Asian trade on fears over surging AI infrastructure costs. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/25/stock-market-today-live-updates.html))
- **OpenAI IPO delay**: Reports that OpenAI is weighing delaying its IPO to 2027 — rather than list now below a $1 trillion valuation — rattled AI-linked equities globally. ([Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4607680-ai-memory-chip-stocks-fall-as-global-peers-slump-after-report-openai-mulling-ipo-delay-to-2027))
- **Hardware price hikes**: Apple raised prices on select MacBooks and iPads by up to $300 (base MacBook Air now $1,299, up from $1,099) and Microsoft will raise Xbox console prices by $100–$150 from August 1, both citing soaring AI-driven memory/chip costs. ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/06/26/apple-microsoft-prices-ai), [Thurrott](https://www.thurrott.com/games/337919/microsoft-announces-up-to-150-price-increases-for-xbox-consoles-starting-on-august-1))
- **RBA holds rates**: The Reserve Bank kept the cash rate at 4.35% after three straight 2026 hikes, with Governor Michele Bullock warning further tightening remains possible if inflation doesn't ease. ([SBS News](https://www.sbs.com.au/news/live-blog/rba-june-2026-interest-rates-decision-live/ntpgs9xc6))
- **Fed/bond market**: The US 10-year yield fell to ~4.38%, a seven-week low, even as the Fed's latest dot plot points to a more hawkish 2026 rate path than previously projected. ([Advisor Perspectives](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/dshort/updates/2026/06/26/treasury-yields-snapshot-june-26-2026))
- **Commodities**: Gold fell toward $4,000/oz, down about 5% for the week, as hawkish Fed signals outweighed support from US–Iran de-escalation; oil retreated further toward pre-conflict levels on eased Middle East supply concerns; iron ore was roughly flat near $100/T.
- **Market breadth**: Despite the tech rout, market breadth improved — by Thursday 63% of S&P 500 stocks traded above their 50-day moving average, up from 50% at the start of June, suggesting rotation into non-tech sectors. ([Schwab](https://www.schwab.com/learn/story/stock-market-update-open))

## Investment Implications
- The AI/chip cost shock and OpenAI IPO delay highlight that the AI trade remains a key swing factor for broad index performance — continued weakness in mega-cap tech could weigh on the S&P 500 and Nasdaq even as breadth elsewhere improves.
- Diverging central bank paths — RBA on hold but warning of more hikes, Fed turning more hawkish — add uncertainty to rate-sensitive assets and currency markets, reflected in AUD/USD weakness and volatile bond yields.
- Falling gold and a weakening Iron Ore complex suggest reduced near-term safe-haven and China-demand-driven commodity support; commodity-linked currencies like the AUD may stay under pressure absent fresh catalysts.

## Key Financial News

- **Record US equity closes:** The S&P 500 crossed 7,500 for the first time on record (+0.77%), while the Dow Jones retook the 50,000 level for the first time since February (+0.74%). The Nasdaq Composite hit a fresh all-time high (+0.88%), led by AI and semiconductor stocks. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-may-14-2026-sp-futures-rise-after-closing-at-record-high), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/live/stock-market-today-dow-retakes-50000-level-sp-500-and-nasdaq-surge-as-ai-trade-roars-back-223230523.html))

- **Nvidia and AI trade roar back:** Nvidia surged more than 4% after the US government approved sales of its H200 chips to several Chinese firms — a policy reversal that investors read as a direct result of the Trump-Xi Beijing summit. The AI trade broadly outperformed, lifting semiconductor and cloud infrastructure names. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/13/stock-market-today-live-updates.html))

- **Cisco beats, surges 13%:** Cisco Systems jumped over 13% after reporting third-quarter results and guidance that beat Wall Street expectations, while simultaneously announcing approximately 4,000 job cuts. The print was widely seen as confirmation that enterprise technology spending remains robust despite macro headwinds. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-may-14-2026-sp-futures-rise-after-closing-at-record-high))

- **Trump-Xi summit in Beijing:** President Trump met with President Xi Jinping to address trade imbalances, the Iran conflict, and Taiwan tensions. The two sides agreed to establish bilateral boards for economic and AI oversight. The diplomatic progress eased geopolitical risk premiums across equities and helped push tech stocks higher. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-13/asian-stocks-to-climb-as-wall-street-hits-new-high-markets-wrap))

- **Hot US CPI:** April CPI rose 3.8% YoY, the highest reading since May 2023, following the elevated PPI print the prior day. Markets have now fully priced out any Fed rate cuts in 2026 and assign roughly a 28% probability to a rate hike by December. The 10-year Treasury yield eased marginally to ~4.45% as equities absorbed the data with relative calm. ([US Treasury](https://home.treasury.gov/resource-center/data-chart-center/interest-rates/TextView?type=daily_treasury_yield_curve&field_tdr_date_value=2026), [Fed H.15](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/h15/))

- **RBA raises for third consecutive meeting:** The Reserve Bank of Australia lifted the official cash rate 25 bps to 4.35% in an 8-1 board vote. Governor Michele Bullock cited underlying CPI at 4.6% YoY and Iran War energy spillovers as key concerns, flagging a further hike likely in August. Australia's Federal Budget (delivered 12 May) was assessed as fiscally neutral and offers little disinflationary relief. ([RBA](https://www.rba.gov.au/media-releases/2026/mr-26-12.html), [CommBank](https://www.commbank.com.au/articles/newsroom/2026/05/rba-may-interest-rates-decision.html))

- **ASX 200 closes modestly higher:** The ASX 200 edged up 0.12% to 8,640.7 in a cautious session, as investors weighed the RBA rate hike and Federal Budget implications against overnight optimism from Wall Street. Resources stocks led gains; rate-sensitive sectors lagged. ([IG AU](https://www.ig.com/au/news-and-trade-ideas/asx-200-afternoon-report--14-may-2026-260514), [ShareCafe](https://www.sharecafe.com.au/2026/05/14/asx-set-to-open-lower-us-markets-hit-record-highs-on-ai-rally/))

## Investment Implications

- **AI-driven rally broadens:** The combination of the Trump-Xi summit (enabling resumed chip sales) and strong Cisco earnings signals that both consumer and enterprise AI demand remains intact. Sustained AI momentum is positive for tech-heavy indexes, but record-high valuations leave little margin for macro disappointment.
- **Inflation and rates headwind for rate-sensitives:** With the RBA at 4.35% and signalling more hikes, and the Fed firm on hold with a hike bias developing, property, utilities, and consumer discretionary sectors in both Australia and the US face ongoing pressure. Defensive positioning in energy and resources may offer relative value.
- **AUD in a tug of war:** The RBA's hawkish stance is supportive of AUD/USD, but slowing global growth signals and uncertainty around US-China trade outcomes could limit upside. Iron ore data divergence across sources adds uncertainty for the near-term outlook for Australian miners.

## Watchlist for Today

- **US-China summit outcomes:** Any detailed communiqué on trade tariffs, chip export rules, or AI governance frameworks could move tech and materials stocks significantly on Friday.
- **Fed speaker commentary:** With CPI at 3.8% and a December hike now carrying non-trivial odds (~28%), any FOMC member remarks that lean more hawkish than the current consensus could steepen the yield curve further.
- **ASX opening reaction:** Watch how Australian banks and property stocks respond to the confirmed RBA hike and any budget-related fiscal detail — the Index's next direction hinges on whether rate-sensitive sectors stabilise.

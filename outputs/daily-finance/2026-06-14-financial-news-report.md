# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-14

## Executive Summary
- President Trump announced late Sunday that a deal to end the US-Iran war is "now complete" — the US naval blockade of Iran is being lifted immediately and the Strait of Hormuz will reopen toll-free, with a formal signing ceremony set for Friday, 19 June in Switzerland.
- Oil prices tumbled on the news: WTI crude fell roughly 4.8% to around $80.80/bbl and Brent dropped about 3.9% to near $83.89/bbl, both touching multi-month lows, while US stock futures jumped (Dow +342 pts/+0.7%, S&P 500 futures +0.9%, Nasdaq 100 futures +1.4%) ahead of Monday's open.
- The de-escalation momentum was complicated by a fresh Israeli strike on a Hezbollah command centre in Beirut on Sunday, which Trump said "should not have happened," underscoring that the broader Lebanon front remains fragile even as the Iran deal text was finalised.

## Key Financial News

- **US-Iran War Deal "Now Complete":** Trump said late Sunday that the agreement with Iran is finalised, with Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif confirming an official signing ceremony will be held Friday, 19 June, in Switzerland. Trump ordered an immediate halt to the US naval blockade of Iran and authorised the toll-free reopening of the Strait of Hormuz ("Let the oil flow!"). ([PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/trump-says-deal-reached-with-iran-and-he-has-authorized-that-naval-blockade-leave-strait-of-hormuz) | [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/14/nx-s1-5858099/us-iran-trump-deal-end-war-hormuz))
- **Oil Slides to Multi-Month Lows:** WTI crude dropped about 4.8% to roughly $80.80/bbl and Brent fell near 3.9% to about $83.89/bbl in the immediate reaction, both at their lowest levels in months, as markets priced in restored crude flows through Hormuz. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/14/oil-price-iran-war-strait-hormuz-peace-deal.html))
- **US Stock Futures Jump Ahead of Monday's Open:** Dow futures rose 342 points (+0.7%), S&P 500 futures gained 0.9%, and Nasdaq 100 futures climbed 1.4% Sunday evening on the deal news; ASX 200 futures pointed to roughly a 0.4% gain at Monday's open. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/14/stock-market-today-live-updates.html))
- **Israeli Strike on Beirut Clouds the Picture:** Israel struck a Hezbollah command centre in Beirut on Sunday, prompting Trump to say "this morning's attack on Beirut should not have happened" and to call on all sides to stand down — a reminder that the Lebanon front remains a wildcard ahead of the 19 June signing. ([CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/live-updates/iran-war-us-trump-peace-deal-agreement/))
- **Gold Extends Its Rally Despite De-escalation:** Gold gained roughly $43/oz to around $4,219, continuing its record-territory run even as the Iran deal reduced near-term geopolitical risk — a sign safe-haven and inflation-hedge demand remains elevated. ([BusinessToday](https://www.businesstoday.in/personal-finance/story/gold-silver-rates-today-june-14-2026-check-latest-prices-for-24k-22k-18k-in-delhi-mumbai-chennai-kolkata-536758-2026-06-14))
- **AUD/USD Holds Near 0.7045:** The Australian dollar was little changed over the weekend versus Friday's close, with FX markets positioning ahead of the RBA's two-day policy meeting starting Monday. ([exchange-rates.org](https://www.exchange-rates.org/exchange-rate-history/aud-usd-2026))
- **Big Central-Bank Week Ahead:** The RBA board meets 15-16 June, with the cash-rate decision due 16 June at 2:30pm AEST — economists are split between a hold at 4.35% and a further hike to 4.60% (Westpac). The Fed's FOMC meets 16-17 June, the first meeting chaired by new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh, with a hold widely expected. ([Finder – RBA Cash Rate](https://www.finder.com.au/rba-cash-rate) | [CNBC – Warsh's first FOMC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/12/warsh-fed-chair-interest-rates.html))

## Investment Implications

- **Risk-On Tilt at Monday's Open, but Fragile:** The futures surge and sharp oil drop point to a positive open across US and Australian equities, but durability depends on whether the Beirut strike triggers retaliation that threatens the 19 June signing.
- **Oil's Slide Could Ease a Key Inflation Input:** A sustained move toward multi-month lows in crude would lower a major cost pressure just as the RBA and the Fed (under new Chair Warsh) weigh their next steps — a tailwind for rate-hold or eventual rate-cut narratives if the de-escalation holds.
- **Gold's Resilience Signals Lingering Hedging Demand:** Bullion's continued advance despite the de-escalation suggests investors are not yet fully pricing in a clean resolution; a smooth 19 June signing could prompt some unwind in gold even as risk assets extend gains.

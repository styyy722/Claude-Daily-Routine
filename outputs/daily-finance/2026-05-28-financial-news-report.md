# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-05-28

## Executive Summary
- S&P 500 and Nasdaq closed at fresh record highs, driven by Snowflake's 36% surge after a landmark $6 billion AWS deal and strong earnings beat rekindled AI software optimism across the sector.
- April PCE inflation rose to 3.8% YoY—a near three-year high—but softer month-on-month prints eased immediate rate hike fears and allowed equity markets to look past the hot headline number.
- Reports of a US-Iran ceasefire extension sent oil prices sharply lower (-4.0%) and weighed on gold (-1.4%), while Australia's softer-than-expected April CPI print fuelled ASX 200 gains and reinforced expectations for an RBA rate pause in June.

## Key Financial News

- **Snowflake AI surge drives tech rally:** SNOW shares surged ~36% to $244.45—its best single session ever—after Q1 FY27 product revenue rose 34% YoY to $1.33B, beating consensus, and the company announced a $6B multi-year Amazon Web Services deal centred on generative and agentic AI integrations. Microsoft, Oracle, and Palantir each gained 3–4%, lifting the broader software sector to a new cycle high. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/snowflake-snow-software-stock-rally.html), [Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/stock-market-today-may-28-182610205.html))
- **April PCE runs hot but MoM softens:** The April PCE price index rose 3.8% YoY (highest since May 2023, up from 3.5% in March); core PCE ticked up to 3.3% YoY (highest since October 2023). Month-on-month prints were softer than expected (headline +0.4% vs +0.5% consensus; core +0.2% vs +0.3%), reducing immediate pressure on the Fed to act aggressively. ([Benzinga](https://www.benzinga.com/markets/macro-economic-events/26/05/52835775/us-pce-inflation-report-april-2026))
- **Geopolitical relief—oil and gold fall sharply:** Reports of a US-Iran ceasefire extension sent WTI crude down 3.97% to $90.16/bbl and gold lower by 1.42% to $4,470.70/oz. The Iran conflict had been a key driver of elevated energy prices and a key upside risk to global inflation over recent months.
- **Australia's April CPI undershoots:** Australia's April monthly CPI indicator showed annual inflation eased to 4.2% (below the 4.4% consensus), down from 4.6% in March. Trimmed mean inflation edged up slightly to 3.4%, but the headline miss reinforced market expectations that the RBA will hold its cash rate at 4.35% at the June meeting.
- **ASX 200 rallies on rate-pause hopes:** Interest rate-sensitive stocks led the ASX 200 0.69% higher to 8,717.7, with the softer CPI print reducing the probability of a near-term RBA hike. Australian gold mining stocks remained under pressure from the lower gold price.
- **AUD/USD weakens:** The Australian dollar slipped 0.53% to 0.7133, pressured by a firmer USD following the hot US PCE headline, despite the softer domestic CPI reading.
- **US 10-year yield edges lower:** The benchmark US Treasury yield dipped to ~4.47% from ~4.50% after softer MoM inflation data, providing modest support to rate-sensitive assets globally.

## Investment Implications

- The Snowflake-led AI software rally confirms that enterprise AI spending cycles remain robust; however, with PCE rising to a near three-year high of 3.8%, the Fed's ability to cut rates remains constrained, keeping the cost-of-capital environment elevated for growth and tech stocks over the medium term.
- The Iran ceasefire narrative may provide temporary relief from energy-driven inflation, but with Middle East tensions unresolved, oil prices and inflation expectations could swing sharply on any breakdown in negotiations—a key tail risk for markets.
- Australia's softer headline CPI strengthens the case for the RBA to pause in June, which could continue to support rate-sensitive ASX sectors such as real estate, utilities, and consumer discretionary; however, the rise in trimmed mean inflation signals that underlying price pressures have not fully eased.

## Watchlist for Today

- **Fed speaker commentary:** Monitor any Federal Reserve statements responding to the April PCE data—any hawkish signals could pressure US equities and lift the USD heading into the long weekend.
- **Iran ceasefire developments:** Fresh updates on US-Iran negotiations will be a key driver of oil prices, gold, and broader geopolitical risk sentiment given the sharp market reaction to ceasefire reports.
- **ASX rate-sensitive sectors:** Watch REITs, utilities, and financials on the ASX as the market continues to price in an RBA pause; any fresh RBA guidance could shift expectations quickly in either direction.

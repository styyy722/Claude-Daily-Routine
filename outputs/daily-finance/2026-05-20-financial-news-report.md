## Key Financial News

- **US Markets Rebound:** Stocks snapped a three-session losing streak on 20 May as oil prices and Treasury yields retreated amid growing hopes that the Middle East conflict could soon be resolved. The S&P 500 added 1.08%, the Nasdaq gained 1.54%, and the Dow crossed 50,000 again. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-nasdaq-dow-jones-sp-500-updates-may-20-2026))
- **Nvidia Delivers Record Quarter:** After the close, Nvidia reported Q1 FY2027 revenue of $81.6 billion (up 85% year-on-year), beating the consensus of $79.2 billion. Data centre revenue reached $75.2 billion. Q2 guidance of $89.1–$92.8 billion also topped Wall Street's $87.3 billion estimate; the company raised its quarterly dividend to $0.25 per share. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/article/nvidia-to-report-q1-earnings-as-chip-competition-grows-191200841.html))
- **SpaceX Files for Historic IPO:** SpaceX submitted its S-1 prospectus under the proposed ticker "SPCX," targeting a ~$1.75 trillion valuation—set to eclipse Saudi Aramco's 2019 record as the world's largest IPO. The company posted $18+ billion in 2025 revenue (with Starlink generating over half) alongside a $4.9 billion loss. A listing date of around 12 June is targeted. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/20/spacex-ipo-live-updates.html) | [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/20/the-spacex-ipo-filing-has-arrived/))
- **RBA Hikes for Third Straight Meeting:** The RBA raised its cash rate by 25 bps to 4.35%, warning that Middle East energy shocks could keep underlying inflation above 3% until late 2027. CBA economists believe the RBA now has room to pause as it monitors the pass-through of three consecutive hikes. ([CommBank](https://www.commbank.com.au/articles/newsroom/2026/05/rba-may-interest-rates-decision.html))
- **ASX 200 Hits 20-Day Low:** The local index fell 108.10 points to 8,496.60 as the RBA hike, elevated global yields, and broad weakness in mining and bank stocks weighed. Tuas Ltd dropped 13.86%; materials and energy sectors led the decline. ([IBTimes AU](https://www.ibtimes.com.au/sp-asx-200-index-20-day-low-market-sell-off-1869179))
- **Treasury Yields Ease:** The US 10-year yield pulled back to ~4.64% from a 16-month high reached earlier in the week, boosting risk appetite in the US session. Japan's 30-year bond yield hit a record high, keeping global long-end yield pressure in focus. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/18/treasury-yields-inflation-bond-rout-oil.html))
- **Commodities Mixed:** Gold held near $4,490.73/oz (+0.19%), supported by safe-haven demand and a softer dollar. Iron ore edged down ~0.19% to ~$110.33/T. Brent crude fell toward ~$110/bbl on Middle East peace optimism, partially easing earlier inflationary pressure from the energy shock.

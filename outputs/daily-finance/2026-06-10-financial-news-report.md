# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-10

## Executive Summary
- US stocks sold off sharply (Dow -1.87%, S&P 500 -1.62%, Nasdaq -1.98%) after May CPI rose 4.2% year-on-year — its highest in three years — with energy prices up 23.5% annually amid Middle East instability.
- US-Iran tensions escalated further as Washington struck Iranian targets near the Strait of Hormuz following Tuesday's downing of a US Apache helicopter; President Trump warned Iran would "have to pay the price" for further action.
- Oil jumped (WTI +3.5% to above $91/bbl) on the escalation while gold tumbled over 3% to below $4,200/oz on a stronger US dollar; the ASX 200 closed nearly flat at 8,604.1 after a volatile session that saw it fall over 1.5% intraday.

## Key Financial News

- **US Inflation Hits Three-Year High:** May CPI rose 4.2% year-on-year, in line with expectations, while core CPI (ex food and energy) rose 2.9% annually but only 0.2% month-on-month, undershooting forecasts. Energy prices were the standout, surging 23.5% year-on-year on Middle East-driven supply concerns. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-wednesday-june-10-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-may-cpi-report-215056384.html))

- **Wall Street Sells Off:** The Dow fell 953.33 points (-1.87%) to 49,918.78, the S&P 500 dropped 1.62% to 7,266.99, and the Nasdaq Composite slid 1.98% to 25,169.50 — the sharpest one-day declines in weeks — as hot inflation data and escalating Middle East risk weighed on sentiment. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-10-2026))

- **US-Iran Conflict Intensifies:** Following Tuesday's downing of a US Army Apache helicopter near the Strait of Hormuz, US forces struck Iranian targets in the area. President Trump said Iran had "taken too long" to negotiate and signalled further American military action may follow, keeping geopolitical risk elevated. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-wednesday-june-10-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-may-cpi-report-215056384.html))

- **Oil Jumps on Supply Risk:** WTI crude rose roughly 3.5% to above $91/bbl and Brent climbed nearly 3% to above $94/bbl as the renewed US-Iran clashes raised concerns over potential disruption to Strait of Hormuz shipping lanes. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-wednesday-june-10-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-may-cpi-report-215056384.html))

- **Gold Slumps Below $4,200:** Bullion fell more than 3% to below $4,200/oz, extending its recent slide, as a stronger US dollar and steady Treasury yields reduced the appeal of the safe-haven metal even amid heightened geopolitical tensions. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-wednesday-june-10-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-may-cpi-report-215056384.html))

- **Treasury Yields Hold Steady:** The US 10-year yield was little changed at ~4.52%, easing from an intraday high of 4.55%, as the CPI report's soft core monthly reading offered some relief that the energy shock has not yet broadly spread into underlying prices. Traders modestly pared back Fed rate-hike bets, though a 25bp December hike remains fully priced in. ([Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/government-bond-yield))

- **ASX 200 Whipsaws to a Flat Close:** The benchmark plunged over 1.5% intraday before recovering to close at 8,604.1 (-1.3 pts, -0.02%), its fourth straight session in the red. Miners led losses on softer commodity prices and risk-off positioning, while consumer staples (Coles, Woolworths), healthcare, real estate and telecoms outperformed. Communications group oOh!media surged over 9% on a ~$765 million Bain Capital takeover approach. NAB's chief economist also signalled the RBA's 4.35% cash rate has likely peaked, bringing forward easing expectations to H1 2027. ([IBTimes AU](https://www.ibtimes.com.au/asx-200-closes-flat-amid-volatile-trading-1870415))

- **Tech Selloff Continues:** Semiconductor stocks declined again as investors weighed AI trade valuations ahead of a wave of mega-IPOs, including SpaceX, OpenAI and Anthropic. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-wednesday-june-10-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-may-cpi-report-215056384.html))

## Investment Implications

- **Stagflation Concerns Are Building:** A three-year-high CPI print combined with escalating Middle East conflict raises the risk of an energy-driven inflation shock just as growth indicators soften, complicating the policy path for incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh ahead of the 16–17 June FOMC meeting.
- **Oil Is Now the Key Cross-Asset Swing Factor:** Unlike Tuesday's muted reaction, WTI's +3.5% jump shows markets are starting to price in genuine Strait of Hormuz disruption risk — a sustained move above $95/bbl could feed directly back into inflation expectations and further pressure equities.
- **Defensive Rotation Is Underway:** The ASX's resilience in staples, healthcare and real estate against a sharp miner selloff, alongside the broader Wall Street tech pullback, suggests investors are favouring lower-beta, less rate-sensitive sectors as uncertainty rises.

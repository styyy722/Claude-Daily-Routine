## Key Financial News

- **Dow hits fresh record:** The DJIA closed at 50,579.70 (+294 pts, +0.58%), marking an all-time record close. The S&P 500 settled at 7,473.47 (+0.37%) and the Nasdaq rose 0.19% to 26,343.97. US markets will be closed Monday 25 May for Memorial Day. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-may-22-2026))
- **Health Care leads the week:** The sector gained 1.19% on the day and approximately 3.4% for the week — its strongest weekly performance since November 2025 — boosted by positive earnings and policy tailwinds. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/stock-market-today-live-updates.html))
- **Fed divided on rate path:** Minutes released on 20 May showed the FOMC voted to hold rates at 3.50–3.75% but three governors dissented in favour of more hawkish language, signalling that further hikes are possible if Middle East-driven energy inflation continues. ([US News](https://www.usnews.com/news/national-news/articles/2026-05-20/fed-minutes-suggest-interest-rate-hikes-are-on-the-table-if-inflation-continues))
- **RBA hikes to 4.35%:** Australia's central bank raised its cash rate target by 25 bps to 4.35% — its third increase in 2026 — citing headline inflation expected to peak at 4.8% in mid-2026 and persistent second-round effects from elevated fuel costs. CBA economists expect a pause for the rest of the year. ([RBA](https://www.rba.gov.au/publications/smp/2026/may/))
- **US 10-year yield holds at 4.57%:** Bond yields remained elevated, up approximately 24 bps over the past month, reflecting sticky inflation expectations and ongoing Fed uncertainty. ([Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/government-bond-yield))
- **AUD/USD softer at 0.7136:** The Australian dollar slipped 0.22% against the USD, weighed by risk-off sentiment around the RBA's hawkish stance and a firm greenback supported by high real US yields.
- **Oil near $97, gold under pressure:** WTI crude hovered around $97.29/bbl as the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz kept energy markets tight. Gold retreated ~0.75% to ~$4,524/oz as rising real yields and USD strength reduced its appeal. Iron ore eased to ~$109.79/T.

## Investment Implications

- The Dow's record close and broad-market gains suggest US equities remain resilient ahead of the Memorial Day break; however, the Fed's hawkish undertone and elevated yields could cap further upside, particularly in rate-sensitive sectors such as Real Estate and Utilities.
- The RBA's third 2026 rate hike is likely to weigh on Australian consumer spending and the housing market in coming months; Australian financials may face margin pressure while miners benefiting from elevated commodity prices could offer relative support to the ASX.
- Persistently high oil prices driven by Middle East supply disruption continue to complicate inflation trajectories globally, making central bank pivots to easing less likely in the near term and maintaining upward pressure on bond yields.

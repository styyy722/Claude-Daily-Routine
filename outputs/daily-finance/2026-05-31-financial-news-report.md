## Key Financial News

- **Nasdaq's best May in years:** The Nasdaq Composite surged 8% in May, its strongest monthly performance in several years, driven by AI-related tech earnings and fading energy-market anxiety. The S&P 500 added ~5% for the month, closing at a fresh record of 7,580.06 on May 29. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/28/stock-market-today-live-updates.html))
- **U.S.–Iran ceasefire MOU awaiting Trump signature:** U.S. and Iranian negotiators reached a 60-day memorandum of understanding to extend the ceasefire and begin nuclear talks, but it requires President Trump's sign-off. Oil markets fell sharply on the news — WTI posted its worst monthly decline since April 2025, shedding ~17% in May. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/oil-falls-over-1percent-on-reports-of-potential-us-iran-ceasefire-deal.html))
- **PCE inflation reinforces Fed hold:** April headline PCE rose 3.8% year-over-year (March: 3.5%), its highest reading in nearly three years. Core PCE came in at 3.3%. Real consumer spending grew just 0.1% month-on-month. The Fed Funds rate stays at 3.50–3.75% with rate cuts now firmly off the table for the near term. ([BEA](https://www.bea.gov/))
- **RBA hikes for a third straight time:** The Reserve Bank of Australia lifted the cash rate by 25 bps to 4.35% at its May meeting in an 8–1 vote, fully reversing its 2025 easing cycle. The RBA cited persistent capacity pressures and fuel-driven inflation from the Middle East conflict. CBA economists believe the RBA can now pause, while Westpac forecasts two additional hikes. ([RBA](https://www.rba.gov.au/media-releases/2026/mr-26-12.html))
- **Bond yields ease from May highs:** The U.S. 10-year Treasury yield settled near 4.44%, retreating from its 4.7% intraday peak earlier in May as oil prices fell and risk sentiment improved. The yield curve remains inverted.
- **AUD/USD holds near 0.72:** The Australian dollar was steady around 0.7182–0.7187, supported by improved risk appetite and stronger iron ore prices, though the elevated U.S. rate outlook continues to cap AUD upside.
- **Commodities diverge:** Gold slipped to ~$4,540/oz amid easing geopolitical fears, while iron ore held near $106.80/T. Coal costs are pressuring Chinese steelmaker margins and may weigh on iron ore demand in June. ([Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/))

## Investment Implications

- With both the Fed (3.75% ceiling) and RBA (4.35%) on extended holds or moving higher, the macro environment remains unfavourable for long-duration fixed income; equity investors should monitor whether tech earnings can sustain the rally.
- A Trump-signed U.S.–Iran MOU would lock in lower oil prices through at least late July, easing cost-push inflation globally and providing central banks — including the RBA — more room to pause without re-igniting energy-driven CPI.
- Iron ore's near-term outlook is fragile: Chinese demand signals are mixed, coal costs are squeezing margins, and any demand slowdown from a higher-rate environment in Australia could weigh on Australian resource equities.

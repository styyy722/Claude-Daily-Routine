# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-05

## Executive Summary
- US markets sold off sharply on Friday as a blowout May jobs report (+172K vs. 85K forecast) revived Federal Reserve rate-hike bets and triggered a massive rotation out of semiconductor and AI-related stocks, with the Nasdaq falling 4.18% for its worst session since April 2025.
- The strong labour data pushed the US 10-year Treasury yield to 4.55%; interest-rate swaps fully priced in a 25bp Fed hike by year-end 2026, a dramatic reversal from rate-cut expectations at the start of the year.
- Chip stocks collectively erased over $1 trillion in market value (Nvidia –6.2%, Micron –6.3%, Marvell –8%), while gold tumbled 3.28% to $4,328/oz — its lowest level of 2026.

## Key Financial News

- **Blowout May Jobs Report Shocks Markets:** The US Bureau of Labor Statistics reported 172,000 nonfarm payrolls added in May 2026 — more than double the consensus estimate of 85,000 — while upward revisions to March and April added a further 93,000 jobs. Unemployment held at 4.3% and average hourly earnings rose 0.3% month-on-month to $37.53/hr. ([BLS](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.nr0.htm) | [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/05/jobs-report-may-2026.html))

- **Fed Rate Hike Fully Priced In by Year-End:** The jobs surprise fundamentally shifted rate expectations. Interest-rate swaps fully priced a 25bp hike by the December 2026 FOMC meeting, with ~60% probability of a move as early as October. The Fed — under Chair Kevin Warsh — had held rates at 3.5%–3.75% after three cuts in 2025; markets now see hiking risk rather than additional easing. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/policy/articles/traders-fully-price-fed-rate-124726318.html))

- **Treasury Yields Surge:** The 10-year Treasury yield climbed to 4.55% — a multi-month high — while the 2-year yield jumped ~13 basis points to 4.17%, its largest single-day jump since the April 2025 tariff shock. The 30-year yield briefly topped 5.19% earlier in May, reflecting ongoing fiscal concerns. ([Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/government-bond-yield))

- **Semiconductor Rout Wipes $1 Trillion From Markets:** Chip stocks suffered their worst session since April 2025. Nvidia fell 6.2% (threatening a ~$300B single-day market-cap loss from its $4.97T valuation), Micron dropped 6.3%, Marvell lost 8%, and AMD, Intel, and Arm also declined broadly. Despite Broadcom posting Q2 revenue of $22.19B (+48% YoY) and Q3 guidance of $29.4B (+84% YoY), the stock fell ~3.8% on a sell-the-news reaction, spilling over across the sector. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-05-2026))

- **Gold Hits 2026 Low:** Gold fell 3.28% to $4,328/oz, its lowest close of 2026, heading for a weekly decline of nearly 4%. A stronger US dollar and rising real yields weighed on the non-yielding metal, though prices remain ~31% above year-ago levels. ([Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/gold))

- **Oil Drops on Weak Chinese Demand:** WTI crude fell approximately 3% to ~$90.30/bbl as Chinese crude imports dropped to their lowest level in 10 years, and US-Iran nuclear talks failed to produce a breakthrough. Softer demand signals overshadowed any supply-side support. ([Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/crude-oil))

- **ASX 200 Falls 0.70%; AUD Weakens:** The Australian sharemarket declined 0.70% on Friday, tracking Thursday's US chip-stock selloff and broader rate-hike repricing. AUD/USD fell to 0.7125 as the greenback strengthened on the back of the strong US employment data. ([Investing.com](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/australia-stocks-lower-at-close-of-trade-spasx-200-down-070-4727860))

## Investment Implications

- **Rate Sensitivity Returns as the Key Theme:** The sharp repricing of Fed expectations — from two cuts to one hike — is a meaningful headwind for growth and rate-sensitive sectors globally, including technology, real estate, and utilities. Duration risk in bond portfolios has risen materially.
- **AI/Semiconductor Valuations Under Scrutiny:** Despite strong fundamental earnings from Broadcom, the sector's negative reaction highlights stretched valuations and that even good results cannot sustain current multiples in a rising-rate environment. Near-term consolidation in chip stocks is likely.
- **AUD and Australian Markets Face Dual Pressure:** A weaker Australian dollar may offer temporary relief for resource exporters, but higher global rates could prompt the RBA to reconsider its own easing bias, creating headwinds for rate-sensitive Australian sectors including property and banks.

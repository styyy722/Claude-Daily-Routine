## Key Financial News

- **Micron hits $1 trillion market cap:** UBS analyst Timothy Arcuri raised Micron's (MU) price target from $535 to $1,625—a 204% hike to the new Street-high—citing HBM4 memory sold out through end-2026, DRAM prices up 58–63%, and Q2 FY2026 revenue of $23.9 billion beating guidance by 28%. Shares surged ~18%, briefly carrying the company into the trillion-dollar club. AMD and Qualcomm also gained. ([Source: American Bazaar](https://americanbazaaronline.com/2026/05/26/micron-reaches-1-trillion-market-cap-following-major-ubs-upgrade-481619/))

- **US-Iran escalation lifts oil:** The US military launched "self-defence strikes" against Iranian vessels and missile sites in southern Iran overnight, rattling markets after Trump had signalled a deal was "largely negotiated." Brent crude touched $100/barrel before easing to ~$99; the Strait of Hormuz remains a focus for energy security. ([Source: Time](https://time.com/article/2026/05/26/us-iran-new-strikes-global-oil-prices-rise-peace-deal-talks-jeopardy/); [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/26/oil-prices-today-brent-wti-iran-trump-hormuz.html))

- **Mixed US session—tech vs. defensives diverge:** S&P 500 and Nasdaq set new all-time highs on semiconductor strength, but the Dow underperformed as healthcare names (UnitedHealth, Merck) and energy stocks retreated. Space stocks Rocket Lab and Firefly Aerospace also surged. ([Source: Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/coverage/stock-market-today/2026/05/26/stock-market-today-may-26-soaring-tech-stocks-push-s-and-p-500-and-nasdaq-to-new-highs/))

- **ASX 200 slides ahead of Federal Budget:** Australian equities fell, led by the major banks on investor concern that the budget would tighten negative gearing rules and capital gains tax settings, dampening mortgage credit growth expectations. Financials were the key drag on the index. ([Source: Market Index](https://www.marketindex.com.au/news/evening-wrap-asx-200-slides-ahead-of-the-federal-budget-banks-down-miners-up-led-by-a-record-close-for-bhp))

- **BHP hits record high:** BHP closed at a record $60.23 (+~2.85%) as COMEX copper futures surged 8.81% month-to-date to US$6.51/lb. Rio Tinto also traded near historic peaks. ([Source: Market Index](https://www.marketindex.com.au/news/evening-wrap-asx-200-slides-ahead-of-the-federal-budget-banks-down-miners-up-led-by-a-record-close-for-bhp))

- **RBA at 4.35% after May hike:** The Reserve Bank raised the cash rate 25bps to 4.35% at its May meeting, the third consecutive increase this year, citing Middle East–driven fuel prices feeding into inflation. CBA economists expect rates on hold for the remainder of 2026. ([Source: RBA](https://www.rba.gov.au/media-releases/2026/mr-26-12.html))

- **Consumer strain in the US:** An analysis noted two-thirds of Americans are cutting back on spending due to high food and gas costs, a potential drag on corporate earnings in consumer-facing sectors. ([Source: Motley Fool](https://www.fool.com/coverage/stock-market-today/2026/05/26/stock-market-today-may-26-soaring-tech-stocks-push-s-and-p-500-and-nasdaq-to-new-highs/))

## Investment Implications

- **AI/memory trade is intensifying:** Micron's milestone and the HBM4 sell-out signal that AI infrastructure spending is structural, not cyclical. Investors may reassess concentration risk as the Nasdaq's gains narrow to a handful of large-cap chip names.
- **Oil remains a two-way risk:** A genuine US-Iran ceasefire could sharply reduce the geopolitical premium in crude, providing relief to inflation and central banks. Renewed escalation or closure of the Strait of Hormuz could push Brent well above $100, reigniting inflation globally and delaying rate cuts.
- **Australian budget policy risk is real:** The Federal Budget's expected changes to negative gearing and capital gains tax are a significant near-term catalyst for domestic financials. Combined with the RBA's 4.35% cash rate, household credit conditions are tightening, posing downside risk to bank earnings.

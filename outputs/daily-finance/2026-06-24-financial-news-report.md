# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-24

## Executive Summary
- US equities were mixed: the Dow rose on rotation into cyclicals while the S&P 500 and Nasdaq slipped ahead of Micron's after-hours earnings.
- Oil extended a fourth straight session of losses, falling below $70/bbl WTI, as optimism over the US-Iran ceasefire and a reopening Strait of Hormuz eased supply fears.
- Gold tumbled over 2.5% to its lowest level since November 2025 on a stronger US dollar and growing bets the Fed (under new Chair Kevin Warsh) will stay hawkish.

## Key Financial News
- The S&P 500 and Nasdaq gave up early gains as declines in energy, communications, and technology weighed on indexes ahead of Micron Technology's earnings release; Micron shares surged after-hours.
- The Dow Jones outperformed, adding 182 points, supported by rotation away from rate-sensitive tech names.
- The Federal Reserve held its target range at 3.50%-3.75% at the June 16-17 FOMC meeting, with new Chair Kevin Warsh reaffirming a commitment to price stability; markets continue to debate the pace of future cuts.
- Oil prices fell for a fourth consecutive session, nearly erasing all gains made since the Middle East conflict began, as Strait of Hormuz tanker traffic resumed and Iraqi production recovered toward 1.5 million b/d.
- Gold dropped more than 2.5% intraday to below $4,000/oz, its weakest level since November 2025, pressured by a firmer US dollar and hawkish Fed rate expectations.
- AUD/USD slipped to 0.6897 as the US dollar broadly strengthened; the Australian dollar remains sensitive to commodity price swings and risk sentiment.
- The ASX 200 closed up 0.24% at 8,808.40, recovering after two rough sessions earlier in the week, even after spending part of the day in negative territory.

## Investment Implications
- The sharp pullback in oil and gold suggests markets are pricing in a durable de-escalation in Middle East tensions, which could ease inflation pressure if sustained.
- A hawkish Fed stance under Chair Warsh, combined with US dollar strength, is weighing on precious metals and could keep pressure on rate-sensitive equity sectors.
- Diverging moves between the Dow (up) and Nasdaq/S&P (down) point to a rotation out of growth/tech and into more cyclical, value-oriented names.

- Further confirmation of Strait of Hormuz tanker traffic and Iraqi oil output, which could extend the slide in crude prices.
- Any follow-through commentary from Fed officials on the pace of policy normalisation under Chair Warsh.

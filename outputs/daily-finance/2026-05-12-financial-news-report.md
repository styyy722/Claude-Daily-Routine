## Key Financial News

- **Hot CPI print weighs on sentiment:** US April CPI rose 3.8% YoY (vs. 3.7% expected) and core CPI rose 2.8% YoY (vs. 2.7% expected), reinforcing concerns that the Federal Reserve's 2% inflation target remains distant. ([24/7 Wall St.](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/05/12/stock-market-live-may-12-2026-sp-500-spy-sinks-on-higher-inflation/))
- **Oil spikes on Iran standoff:** WTI crude surged above $101/barrel after Trump rejected Iran's latest peace proposal, with fears growing that the Strait of Hormuz could remain effectively closed for an extended period. ([MSN Markets](https://www.msn.com/en-in/money/markets/us-stock-market-today-may-12-2026-oil-price-surge-inflation-fears-drag-wall-street-s-p-500-down-0-6-nasdaq-0-9-lower/ar-AA2312NI))
- **Intraday recovery:** US equities fell as much as 0.6–0.9% mid-session before recovering. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 ultimately closed modestly higher, supported by a late-session tech rebound. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/latest-news/stock-market-today-may-12-2026-updates))
- **Rate hike bets build:** With inflation running above target and energy prices elevated, some traders are now pricing in a possible Federal Reserve rate hike before year-end, pushing the 10-year Treasury yield to a one-week high of ~4.42%.
- **Chipmakers and AI stocks hammered intraday:** Intel fell 4.7%, Micron dropped 4%, and CoreWeave slumped 8% at session lows before partial recoveries. Nvidia remains on watch ahead of its May 20 earnings report, with Wells Fargo and Susquehanna reiterating bullish ratings.
- **ASX 200 slips, mining surges:** Australia's benchmark index edged down 0.20% to 8,684.40, but the S&P/ASX 300 Metals & Mining index surged 2.74%, driven by higher iron ore, gold, and copper prices. ([Market Index](https://www.marketindex.com.au/))
- **Defensive flows accelerate:** Approximately $22 billion flowed into dividend-focused ETFs in Q1 2026, underscoring investor appetite for income and stability amid ongoing macro uncertainty.

## Investment Implications

- Persistent above-target inflation combined with elevated energy prices reduces the probability of near-term Fed rate cuts and may continue to pressure rate-sensitive sectors such as technology and real estate investment trusts.
- The oil price spike benefits Australian energy producers but adds to input cost pressures for domestic businesses; the AUD/USD decline to 0.7223 may partially offset commodity gains for unhedged exporters.
- Gold's mild pullback despite the inflationary and geopolitical backdrop suggests near-term profit-taking after recent highs at ~$4,700+/oz; further Middle East escalation could reignite bullion demand.

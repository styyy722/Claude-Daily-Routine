# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-21

## Executive Summary
- US markets were closed Friday, June 19 for the Juneteenth holiday; the last session was Thursday, June 18, when the S&P 500 and Nasdaq rallied as chip strength and US-Iran diplomatic progress offset a hawkish Fed.
- Over the weekend, Iran's IRGC declared the Strait of Hormuz closed (Saturday, June 20), citing Israeli strikes in Lebanon as a violation of the recent US-Iran ceasefire memorandum; US Central Command disputed an actual shutdown, reporting 55 merchant ships and over 17 million barrels of oil still transited the strait on Saturday.
- The ASX 200 fell 0.92% on Friday to cap a volatile week, as a stronger US dollar and falling metals prices weighed on miners; with no Sunday trading, Monday's Asia-Pacific open is set to be dominated by the Hormuz headlines and their effect on oil and risk sentiment.

## Key Financial News
- New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's first FOMC meeting (June 16-17) held rates at 3.50%-3.75% but dropped the dot plot's last 2026 rate-cut projection, with futures markets now pricing a 66% chance of at least one hike before year-end — a hawkish surprise that briefly sold off stocks and pushed bond yields sharply higher.
- US equities recovered Thursday (June 18) as tech and chip stocks led a rebound, with the S&P 500 up 1.08% and Nasdaq up 1.91%, helped by optimism over a US-Iran memorandum of understanding reportedly tied to reopening the Strait of Hormuz.
- Iran's IRGC announced Saturday (June 20) that it had shut the Strait of Hormuz, blaming Israeli strikes in southern Lebanon (which Lebanese authorities said killed at least 16 people) for violating the ceasefire framework; the US disputed that the strait was actually closed.
- The ASX 200 slipped 0.92% Friday to 8,828.7, with the index managing only a sub-0.3% gain for the week after a four-day rally tied to the earlier US-Iran peace deal unwound as metals prices fell and the US dollar strengthened.
- AUD/USD weakened to around 0.6989 (-0.35%) as the US dollar climbed broadly on the more hawkish Fed outlook, with the Aussie down roughly 2.25% over the past month despite remaining up over 8% year-on-year.
- Gold eased to roughly $4,151.74/oz (-1.38%), pulling back from recent highs as a stronger dollar and higher real yields weighed on the metal, though it remains up over 23% year-on-year.
- Iron ore held near $101.14/T (-0.14%), pressured for the week by global growth concerns and the firmer greenback, a headwind for ASX-listed miners.

## Investment Implications
- The Fed's hawkish pivot under Warsh raises the cost-of-capital outlook for risk assets; investors should expect continued sensitivity in equities and bonds to incoming inflation and labor data ahead of the next FOMC meeting.
- The Strait of Hormuz closure claim, even if not yet fully disrupting physical oil flows, raises tail risk for energy prices; a sustained disruption would be inflationary and could complicate the Fed's hiking calculus.
- AUD weakness and softer iron ore are a near-term headwind for Australian resources exposure, while a hawkish US dollar backdrop generally pressures commodity-linked currencies.

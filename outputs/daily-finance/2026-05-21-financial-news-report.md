# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-05-21

- Nvidia posted a blowout Q1 FY27 beat ($81.6B revenue, +85% YoY) but US tech stocks sold off as rising Treasury yields (10-yr: 4.59%) and hawkish FOMC minutes overshadowed strong earnings — a classic "sell the news" session.

## Key Financial News

- **Nvidia Q1 FY27 (announced May 20 after close):** Revenue of $81.62B (+85% YoY, +20% QoQ) beat consensus of $79.2B; Data Center revenue surged to $75.2B (+92% YoY). EPS of $1.87 (non-GAAP) beat the $1.78 estimate. NVIDIA announced an additional $80B share buyback and raised its quarterly dividend from $0.01 to $0.25/share. Despite the beat, US tech stocks sold off as the AI rally failed to extend — reflecting stretched valuations and "buy the rumour, sell the news" dynamics. ([SEC Filing](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001045810/000104581026000051/q1fy27pr.htm), [Kiplinger](https://www.kiplinger.com/investing/live/nvidia-earnings-live-updates-and-commentary-may-2026))

- **FOMC minutes (late-April meeting) released May 21:** The minutes showed a growing number of Federal Reserve policymakers open to rate hikes later in 2026, as elevated oil prices from the Iran conflict threatened to worsen inflation. The Fed held rates at 3.50%–3.75% at the April meeting — Jerome Powell's likely final FOMC meeting as Chair — but hawks are gaining ground. The release pushed US 10-year Treasury yields to 4.59%. ([Federal Reserve](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomccalendars.htm))

- **Australia April employment shocker:** The economy lost 18,600 jobs in April (vs. consensus +15,000), and unemployment rose to 4.5% — its highest level since November 2021. Rate futures fell sharply: only 1bp of hikes priced for June, with the RBA terminal rate falling from 4.85% to ~4.60%. ASX 200 surged 1.61% to 8,633 on relief for rate-sensitive sectors. ([IG AU](https://www.ig.com/au/news-and-trade-ideas/asx-200-afternoon-report--21-may-2026-260521))

- **RBA: Third consecutive rate hike to 4.35%:** The Reserve Bank raised the cash rate 25bp to 4.35% in May (8-1 board split), citing inflation remaining above target at 4.6% (year to March) and upside risks from Middle East energy prices. However, the RBA signalled scope to pause, with CBA economists noting policy is now restrictive enough to monitor data. ([RBA Media Release](https://www.rba.gov.au/media-releases/2026/mr-26-08.html), [CBA](https://www.commbank.com.au/articles/newsroom/2026/05/rba-may-interest-rates-cba-economists-analysis.html))

- **Iran conflict and oil volatility:** Iran's Supreme Leader directed that near-weapons-grade uranium must not be exported, intensifying fears over the Strait of Hormuz and keeping WTI crude near $99.5/bbl. An unverified mid-session report of a US-Iran draft resolution briefly lifted the Dow and Russell 2000 before being disputed. Oil's proximity to $100 continues to fuel global inflation concerns. ([The Street](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-may-21-2026))

- **Intuit (INTU) –21%:** TurboTax and QuickBooks parent Intuit plunged 21% after announcing it would cut approximately 17% of its workforce — one of the sharpest single-day falls in the S&P 500 for the session.

- **Eli Lilly (LLY) +1.05%:** Shares rose after the drugmaker announced its next-generation obesity drug cleared a pivotal Phase 3 clinical trial, reinforcing Lilly's dominant position in the GLP-1 market.

## Investment Implications

- The Nvidia "sell the news" reaction, combined with FOMC hawks gaining ground, suggests near-term headwinds for high-multiple US tech and AI names; investors may rotate toward value, small caps (Russell 2000 +2.56%), or international markets.
- Australia's soft jobs data is a material positive for domestic rate-sensitive sectors — REITs, banks, consumer discretionary, and utilities may benefit as the market reprices a lower RBA terminal rate (~4.60% vs. 4.85% expected a month ago).
- Oil near $100 and geopolitical uncertainty in the Middle East remain a stagflationary risk globally. Energy equities and commodity exposures may serve as partial hedges; portfolio resilience should be assessed given simultaneous Fed hawkishness and high energy costs.

- **RBA follow-up communications:** With the cash rate now at 4.35% and soft jobs data in hand, any RBA governor commentary on the pause signal will be closely watched by AUD and ASX traders.
- **US-Iran diplomatic developments:** Yesterday's bogus peace-deal report caused intraday volatility. Any verified official signals from Washington or Tehran on a ceasefire or Strait of Hormuz reopening will sharply move oil, gold, and equities.

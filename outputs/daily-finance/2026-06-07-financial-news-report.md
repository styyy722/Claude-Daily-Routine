# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-07

*Note: 7 June 2026 is a Sunday — US, Australian and most global equity, bond and commodity markets were closed. This report summarises the most recent close (Friday, 6 June US time / Friday, 5 June AEST), weekend developments, and the outlook for the week ahead. Monday 8 June is also a public holiday in most Australian states (King's Birthday), so the ASX remains shut.*

## Executive Summary
- Markets enter the week still digesting Friday's blowout US May jobs report (+172K vs. ~85K forecast), which flipped rate expectations from cuts to hikes and triggered a ~$1 trillion rout in chip stocks (Nasdaq –4.18%, its worst day since April 2025).
- Over the weekend, the Strait of Hormuz crisis remained the dominant geopolitical risk: Iran says it will "completely" block the strait and has paused indirect talks with Washington over a 60-day ceasefire MOU, keeping oil markets on edge after last week's volatile swings.
- The week ahead is headlined by US CPI (Wednesday) — the last major inflation print before incoming Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's first FOMC meeting on 16–17 June, where he is expected to scrap the "dot plot" and reduce forward guidance — alongside Australian consumer/business confidence data on Tuesday.

## Key Financial News

- **US Markets Close the Week Sharply Lower on Jobs Shock:** Friday's session saw the S&P 500 fall 2.64% to 7,383.74, the Nasdaq tumble 4.18% to 25,709.43 (its worst day since April 2025), and the Dow shed 695 points (-1.35%) to 50,866.78, after a much-stronger-than-expected May payrolls report (+172K vs. +85K consensus) revived bets on Fed rate hikes rather than cuts. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/04/stock-market-today-live-updates.html) | [BLS](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.nr0.htm))

- **Strait of Hormuz Standoff Continues Over the Weekend:** Iran has vowed to "completely" block the Strait of Hormuz and suspended indirect ceasefire negotiations with the US in protest over alleged violations, even as the two sides were reported to have "mostly agreed" on a 60-day truce framework pending President Trump's sign-off. UBS notes crude loadings inside the Gulf remain "extremely low," with little sign of a near-term improvement in vessel traffic. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/01/iran-us-negotiations-strait-of-hormuz.html) | [Wikipedia – 2026 Strait of Hormuz crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Strait_of_Hormuz_crisis))

- **Fed Rate-Hike Bets Fully Priced for Year-End:** Interest-rate swaps now fully price a 25bp Fed hike by December 2026 — a sharp reversal from rate-cut expectations earlier in the year — after the jobs surprise pushed the 10-year Treasury yield to ~4.55%, a multi-month high, and the 2-year yield up roughly 13bps in its biggest single-day jump since the April 2025 tariff shock. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/policy/articles/traders-fully-price-fed-rate-124726318.html) | [Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/government-bond-yield))

- **Incoming Fed Chair Warsh Set to Scrap the Dot Plot:** With his first FOMC meeting as chair scheduled for 16–17 June, Kevin Warsh is reportedly preparing to abstain from the quarterly "dot plot" and may move to retire the framework altogether, alongside fewer post-meeting press conferences — a deliberate break from forward guidance that he has called a "regime change in the conduct of policy." Morgan Stanley flags the meeting as a major underpriced risk for currency markets. ([Seeking Alpha](https://seekingalpha.com/news/4600194-warsh-may-abstain-from-dot-plot-at-june-meeting---report) | [CFR](https://www.cfr.org/articles/what-to-expect-from-kevin-warshs-fed-in-the-first-100-days))

- **Chip Stocks Shed ~$1 Trillion in Friday's Rout:** Semiconductor names bore the brunt of the sell-off — Nvidia fell 6.2%, Micron 6.3% and Marvell 8% — even as Broadcom posted Q2 revenue of $22.19B (+48% YoY) and guided Q3 revenue to $29.4B (+84% YoY); the stock still slid roughly 3.8% on a "sell-the-news" reaction that spilled across the sector. ([TheStreet](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-05-2026))

- **Gold Slides to a 2026 Low; Oil Remains Volatile but Off Its Peaks:** Gold fell roughly 3.3% on Friday to about $4,328/oz — its weakest level of the year and down close to 4% on the week — as a firmer US dollar and rising real yields weighed on the metal. WTI crude eased to around $88.50/bbl, roughly 20% below 2026 highs on hopes for an Iran ceasefire, though prices remain prone to sharp swings on Hormuz headlines (oil jumped over 7% on a single prior announcement that Iran would block the strait). ([Trading Economics – Gold](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/gold) | [CNBC – Oil](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/29/oil-prices-iran-ceasefire-us-trump-strait-hormuz-energy-costs.html))

- **ASX Heads Into a Holiday-Shortened Week on the Back Foot:** The ASX 200 fell about 0.7% on Friday to roughly 8,625, finishing the week down close to 1% — its first weekly decline in three weeks — as materials and financials lagged amid a four-week slide in iron ore (now near US$102/tonne, down from ~US$112) and cautious commentary on banks and housing. The market is shut Monday 8 June for the King's Birthday public holiday in most states. ([IG AU – Week Ahead](https://www.ig.com/au/news-and-trade-ideas/week-ahead--8-june-2026-260605) | [Trading Economics – Iron Ore](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/iron-ore))

## Investment Implications

- **Repriced Rate Path Keeps Pressure on Duration and Growth Assets:** With markets now leaning toward a Fed hike rather than a cut by year-end, rate-sensitive sectors — technology, real estate, utilities — and long-duration bond holdings face a tougher backdrop until incoming Chair Warsh's communication style and policy bias become clearer after the 16–17 June meeting.
- **Energy and Safe-Haven Trades Remain Headline-Driven:** With the Strait of Hormuz standoff unresolved and ceasefire talks stalled, oil and gold are likely to stay highly sensitive to single headlines out of Tehran and Washington — investors should expect sharp, fast moves in either direction rather than a settled trend.
- **Australian Equities Face a Cross-Current of Weak Commodities and Cautious Domestic Data:** A four-week slide in iron ore is weighing on miners, while soft sentiment toward banks and housing ahead of this week's confidence surveys suggests the ASX may continue to lag global peers, even as a softer AUD offers some offsetting support to exporters.

## Watchlist for the Week Ahead (from 8 June 2026)

- **US CPI (Wednesday 10 June):** The last major inflation reading before Warsh's debut FOMC meeting — a hot print would reinforce hike bets and could extend the bond and equity sell-off; a softer number could offer markets some relief.
- **Strait of Hormuz Headlines:** Any escalation or breakthrough in US–Iran ceasefire talks will likely drive outsized, fast moves in oil, gold and risk sentiment — watch for official statements from Tehran, Washington and shipping/insurance markets.
- **Australian Confidence Data and RBA Positioning (Tuesday 9 June):** Westpac consumer confidence and NAB business confidence will be the first readings to capture reaction to the 12 May Federal Budget; weak prints, alongside a market that is now pricing modest RBA tightening for 2026, could add to pressure on rate-sensitive ASX sectors.

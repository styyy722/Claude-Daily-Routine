# Daily Financial News Report – 2026-06-18

## Executive Summary
- US equity markets rebounded sharply on Thursday as semiconductor stocks extended gains and investors reconsidered the hawkish signal from the Federal Reserve's June meeting; the S&P 500 rose ~1% and the Nasdaq 100 surged ~2.3%.
- The Fed held rates steady but nearly half of FOMC members projected at least one rate hike in 2026, pushing bond yields higher and weighing on the ASX 200, which fell 0.6% amid the cautious global mood.
- Oil slid toward $75/bbl as the US-Iran interim peace agreement — reopening the Strait of Hormuz — eased supply-risk premiums, while gold retreated ~1% from its recent highs as the dollar strengthened on higher-for-longer rate expectations.

## Key Financial News

- **Fed holds, signals possible hike:** The Federal Reserve, under new Chairman Kevin Warsh, held the federal funds rate unchanged at its June FOMC meeting but the dot plot was revised sharply higher — with nearly half of members projecting at least one rate hike in 2026, citing upward pressure on core inflation. Warsh declined to submit personal projections, maintaining his stance against explicit forward guidance. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/16/stock-market-today-live-updates.html))
- **Chip stocks lead rebound:** After two sessions of steep losses following the hawkish Fed signal, AI-related semiconductor stocks roared back on Thursday, driving Nasdaq 100 gains of 2.3% and pulling the broader S&P 500 higher. The AI trade re-ignited as investors viewed the selloff as overdone. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/live/stock-market-today-dow-sp-500-nasdaq-climb-with-focus-on-iran-deal-fed-hike-path-230530576.html))
- **Iran deal dampens oil:** The US and Iran digitally signed an interim peace agreement that includes reopening the Strait of Hormuz to oil tanker traffic. WTI crude fell toward $75/bbl — near a multi-month low — as the geopolitical risk premium that had elevated prices unwound. ([FX Daily Report](https://fxdailyreport.com/wti-crude-oil-price-analysis-for-june-18-2026/))
- **ASX 200 falls on hawkish Fed:** The Australian share market slipped 55 points (0.6%) to close around 8,911, snapping a four-session winning streak. The hawkish Fed signal, combined with Trump's warning that the Iran ceasefire was "not final," weighed on risk appetite domestically. ([Discovery Alert](https://discoveryalert.com.au/asx-falls-fed-holds-rates-hawkish-hold-june-2026/))
- **AUD/USD softens:** The Australian dollar edged down to approximately US$0.701 as the prospect of further US rate hikes supported the dollar broadly. The RBA itself held its cash rate at 4.35% earlier in the week, limiting AUD upside.
- **Gold posts "Fed hangover":** Spot gold fell roughly 1% to around $4,246/oz, weighed down by stronger real yields and a firmer US dollar after the Fed's hawkish dot plot. Gold had been trading above $4,300 earlier in the week and above $4,500 a month prior. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/personal-finance/investing/article/gold-prices-today-thursday-june-18-2026-prices-feeling-a-fed-hangover-despite-iran-peace-deal-110614912.html))
- **Iron ore weakens:** Iron ore futures in China declined to around 747.50 CNY/T (~$103/T), falling approximately 1.9% on the day, as persistent weak demand from the steel sector continued to pressure prices. ([Trading Economics](https://tradingeconomics.com/commodity/iron-ore-cny))

## Investment Implications

- The Fed's hawkish shift introduces a higher-for-longer rate environment that is likely to sustain pressure on rate-sensitive sectors (utilities, real estate) and keep the yield curve elevated; quality growth stocks that can absorb higher discount rates may see selective flows return after the dip.
- The Iran peace deal reduces a key oil-supply risk, but it also removes a significant geopolitical premium — energy stocks and ASX resources names exposed to oil may face headwinds, while lower energy costs could provide a modest tailwind for industrials and airlines.
- For Australian investors, the combination of a soft AUD and a hawkish Fed suggests continued caution around foreign-currency-denominated assets; domestically, the RBA's steady hand limits rate-surprise risk but also caps the AUD's recovery prospects near-term.

## Watchlist for Today

- **Fed communications:** Any speeches by Chairman Warsh or FOMC members following the June meeting could clarify the timing and conditions for a potential 2026 rate hike — watch for shifts in tone that move bond yields.
- **Iron ore and ASX mining stocks:** Continued softness in Chinese steel demand is weighing on iron ore; monitor BHP, RIO and FMG for price action and any demand signals out of China's infrastructure pipeline.

## Key Financial News

- **US equities closed higher on Friday.** The Dow Jones rose 0.58% to 50,579, the S&P 500 gained 0.37% to 7,473, and the Nasdaq Composite added 0.19% to 26,343. The Dow has now recaptured 50,000 following its earlier drawdown during peak Iran-war anxiety. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/13/stock-market-today-live-updates.html))
- **Iran-US ceasefire remains fragile.** President Trump declared the truce "on life support" after Tehran submitted an "unacceptable" counter-proposal, keeping energy markets on edge and inflation expectations elevated. Oil rallied more than 50% since the outbreak of hostilities. ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/live/stock-market-today-us-stock-futures-mixed-oil-dips-as-hopes-emerge-for-an-end-to-middle-east-hostilities-002656744.html))
- **RBA raised the cash rate to 4.35% (+25 bps)** at its May board meeting — its third consecutive hike in 2026. The Bank cited persistent inflation driven partly by higher energy costs but left the door open to further tightening. CBA economists expect the RBA to pause for the remainder of 2026. ([CBA Newsroom](https://www.commbank.com.au/articles/newsroom/2026/05/rba-may-interest-rates-cba-economists-analysis.html), [SBS News](https://www.sbs.com.au/news/live-blog/rba-may-2026-rates-decision-live/f5uegtd4q))
- **ASX 200 fell ~0.36% ahead of the Federal Budget.** Banks led the decline as investors worried about potential changes to negative gearing and capital gains tax rules. The budget, delivered Friday evening, did include CGT reform proposals. ([Market Index](https://www.marketindex.com.au/news/evening-wrap-asx-200-slides-ahead-of-the-federal-budget-banks-down-miners-up-led-by-a-record-close-for-bhp))
- **Copper surged to a record high above US$6.45/lb** on COMEX, powering a strong session for the ASX materials sector. BHP set a record close, while Rio Tinto (+3.1%), South32 (+3.6%), Sandfire Resources (+3.1%), and Alcoa (+3.9%) also posted significant gains. ([Market Index](https://www.marketindex.com.au/news/evening-wrap-asx-200-slides-ahead-of-the-federal-budget-banks-down-miners-up-led-by-a-record-close-for-bhp))
- **US 10-year Treasury yield eased to ~4.56%**, retreating from a one-week high. Inflation concerns persist: the April US PPI grew at an annualised rate of ~6%, keeping pressure on the Fed. ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/treasury-yields-resume-climb-as-traders-monitor-inflation-risks.html))
- **AUD/USD weakened to 0.7118 (-0.44%)**, weighed down by risk-off flows from geopolitical tension, the relative hawkishness of the Fed versus an expected RBA pause, and budget uncertainty.

## Investment Implications

- **Australian miners are well-positioned** given record copper and elevated iron ore prices. However, broader ASX gains may be limited by higher domestic rates, budget-related fiscal tightening, and weakness in the financial sector from CGT and negative gearing changes.
- **US equity momentum is intact but geopolitical-dependent.** A credible Iran deal could sharply reduce oil prices, ease inflation expectations, and trigger a broader risk rally. The absence of a deal keeps stagflation concerns alive.
- **Elevated US yields and persistent inflation** are a headwind for duration-sensitive assets. With the Fed facing a deteriorating inflation picture, the path for rate cuts in 2026 remains uncertain.

## Watchlist for Today

- **Australian Federal Budget fallout**: Markets will fully price the budget on the Monday open (May 25). Focus on CGT reform details, housing-market measures, infrastructure commitments, and any changes affecting bank margins or mortgage demand.
- **Iran nuclear talks**: Any signal from Washington or Tehran on ceasefire progress — or breakdown — could move WTI oil by several dollars and ripple into inflation and equity markets globally.
- **Fed communication**: Watch for Fed speakers clarifying the rate path following the elevated April PPI print; any hawkish pivot could push US yields higher and strengthen the USD against the AUD further.
